Easy Lamb Tagine

Easy-Lamb-Tagine • Serves 4–6 • Lamb

This fruity, Moroccan-style lamb tagine is built as an easy one-pot: browned lamb, carrots, chickpeas, and dried apricots simmer gently in a ras el hanout tomato base. It’s the kind of make-ahead dish that tastes even better a day or two later.

Ingredients

  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 500g lean diced lamb
  • 1 large onion, roughly chopped
  • 2 large carrots, quartered lengthways and cut into chunks
  • 2 garlic cloves, finely chopped
  • 2 tbsp ras-el-hanout spice mix
  • 400g can chopped tomato
  • 400g can chickpeas, rinsed and drained
  • 200g dried apricots
  • 600ml chicken stock

To serve

  • 120g pack pomegranate seeds
  • 2 large handfuls coriander, roughly chopped

Method

1. Brown the lamb and vegetables

  1. Heat the oven to 180C/160C fan/gas 4.
  2. Heat the olive oil in a casserole and brown the lamb on all sides. Lift out to a plate.
  3. Add the onion and carrots to the pan and cook for 2–3 minutes until golden.
  4. Stir in the garlic and cook for 1 minute more.

2. Build the tagine base

  1. Stir in the ras el hanout and chopped tomatoes, and season lightly.
  2. Return the lamb to the pan along with the chickpeas and dried apricots.
  3. Pour over the chicken stock, stir, and bring to a simmer.

3. Bake

  1. Cover the casserole and place in the oven for 1 hour.
  2. Check the lamb; if it is still a little tough, give it 20 minutes more until tender.

4. Serve

  1. Let the tagine rest briefly so it is not piping hot.
  2. Scatter with pomegranate seeds and chopped coriander.
  3. Serve with couscous or rice.
Spice note: Ras el hanout is a North African blend often including cardamom, cinnamon, clove, coriander, cumin, nutmeg, turmeric, and black pepper. You can use your own mix of these if needed.
