Lamb, Squash & Apricot Tagine

Lamb-Squash-and-Apricot-Tagine • Serves 4 • Lamb

A classic oven-baked Moroccan-style tagine with lamb, butternut squash, and dried apricots, built on ras el hanout and ground coriander, then finished with lemon zest and fresh coriander.

Ingredients

  • 2 tbsp oil
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 2 garlic cloves, finely chopped
  • 1 tbsp ras el hanout
  • 1 tsp ground coriander
  • 600g lamb leg, diced into 2cm pieces, excess fat trimmed
  • 200g butternut squash, diced
  • 200g soft dried apricots
  • 400g can chopped tomatoes
  • 500ml lamb or beef stock
  • Zest of 1 lemon
  • Small bunch coriander
  • Couscous and natural yogurt, to serve

Method

1. Start the base

  1. Heat the oven to 200C/180C fan/gas 6.
  2. Heat the oil in a flameproof casserole dish.
  3. Add the onion and cook for 5 minutes until softened.
  4. Add the garlic, ras el hanout, and ground coriander, and cook for a couple of minutes more, stirring to prevent catching.

2. Build the tagine

  1. Add the lamb, squash, and apricots to the casserole.
  2. Pour over the chopped tomatoes and stock.
  3. Season well and bring to the boil.

3. Bake

  1. Put the lid on the casserole and transfer to the oven.
  2. After 1 hour, stir the tagine.
  3. Return to the oven, uncovered, for a further 30 minutes.

4. Finish and serve

  1. Check the seasoning.
  2. Sprinkle over the lemon zest and chopped coriander.
  3. Serve in warm bowls with couscous and a spoonful of yogurt.
Timing note: Like most tagines, this is even better the next day after the flavors have had time to settle and mingle.
