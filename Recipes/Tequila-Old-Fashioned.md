# Tequila Old Fashioned

**Makes:** 1 cocktail

---

## Ingredients

- 2 oz reposado tequila (reposado works beautifully here — the oak aging complements the chocolate and vanilla)
- ¼ oz vanilla syrup (store-bought or homemade)
- 2–3 dashes chocolate bitters (Bittermens Xocolatl Mole Bitters are excellent for this)
- Large ice cube
- Orange peel, for garnish
- Optional: pinch of smoked sea salt to rim or add a tiny pinch to the glass

---

## Instructions

Add the tequila, vanilla syrup, and chocolate bitters to a mixing glass filled with ice. Stir for about 30 seconds until well chilled and properly diluted — stirring rather than shaking keeps it silky and clear.

Strain over a large single ice cube in a rocks glass.

Express an orange peel over the drink by giving it a firm twist to release the oils, then run it around the rim and drop it in (or perch it on the ice).

---

## Tips

- Reposado is the sweet spot for this drink, but añejo will give you something richer and more dessert-like.
- Avoid blanco — it's too bright and grassy to pair well with the chocolate and vanilla notes.
- The smoked salt is optional but genuinely elevates it, adding a subtle complexity that ties everything together.
