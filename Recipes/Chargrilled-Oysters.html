New Orleans Chargrilled Oysters

Seafood • Drago's-Style • 10 min prep • 5–7 min cook • Serves 4

The original from Drago's in Metairie — oysters on the half shell blasted over a screaming-hot grill with garlic butter, herbs, and a snow of Parmesan and Romano. The flare-ups are the point. Serve with French bread and no apologies.

The Garlic Butter

  • 1 lb (4 sticks) unsalted butter, softened
  • 8 cloves garlic, very finely minced
  • 1 tbsp fresh oregano, finely chopped (or 1 tsp dried)
  • 1 tsp black pepper
  • ½ tsp cayenne
  • ½ tsp kosher salt

Blend everything together. Make ahead and refrigerate — it keeps for a week and is great on everything.

For the Grill

  • 24 fresh oysters, on the half shell
  • ½ cup grated Parmesan and Romano, mixed
  • Leidenheimer's French bread for serving

Instructions

  1. Fire up the grill to screaming hot. Charcoal is best; gas works. You need direct, intense heat.
  2. Place oysters flat-side up on the grate directly over the flame. They should sit in their shells holding their natural liquor.
  3. Drop a generous tablespoon of garlic butter onto each oyster. It will melt and drip into the fire, causing flare-ups — that's the point. The smoke and flame are the flavor.
  4. When the butter and oyster liquid begins to bubble vigorously around the edges of the shell (about 2 minutes), they're almost done.
  5. Sprinkle the Parmesan/Romano blend over each oyster. Let it melt and just start to brown — another 30–60 seconds.
  6. Pull immediately and serve with Leidenheimer's French bread. The bread is not optional — you need it to sop the butter.

Tips

  • Keep oysters deep-side down until the heat pops them, or shuck and place on the grate with liquid intact.
  • The flare-up from dripping butter chars the oyster slightly at the edges — that's the whole point of "chargrilled."
  • More garlic than you think is right. Then add more.
  • Drago's uses a butter-to-garlic ratio that would alarm most people. Don't be shy.
Note: Charcoal gives you real smoke and char. Gas works but doesn't have the same drama. Either way — hot, fast, and generous with the butter.
