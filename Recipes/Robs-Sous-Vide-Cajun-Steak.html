Rob’s Sous Vide Cajun Steak

Thick-cut steak cooked edge-to-edge in the sous vide bath, then seared hard in cast iron for a proper crust.

Sous Vide Cast-Iron Finish Medium-Rare at 129°F

Ingredients

  • 1 to 2 steaks, about 1 1/2 to 2 inches thick
  • Ribeye, strip, or filet all work
  • 1 to 2 tablespoons salt-free Cajun seasoning
  • Kosher salt, 1/2 to 3/4 teaspoon per pound of steak
  • 1 tablespoon avocado oil
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 2 cloves garlic, smashed
  • 1 sprig fresh thyme or rosemary, optional

Instructions

1. Season the steaks

Pat the steaks dry.

Season all over with Rob’s Cajun seasoning.

Then season with kosher salt separately, using about 1/2 to 3/4 teaspoon per pound of steak.

2. Bag for sous vide

Place the steaks in a vacuum bag or zip-top freezer bag.

Seal the bag.

3. Sous vide

Cook in a water bath at 129°F for medium-rare.

Cook for 2 to 3 hours depending on thickness.

4. Dry the steaks well

Remove the steaks from the bag and pat them very dry with paper towels.

This matters — dry surface means a better crust.

5. Sear

Heat a cast-iron skillet until very hot.

Add the oil, then sear the steaks about 45 to 60 seconds per side.

Add the butter, garlic, and herbs if using, and baste briefly.

6. Rest and serve

Let rest about 5 minutes, then serve.

Finish

After slicing, sprinkle with:

  • A tiny extra pinch of kosher salt
  • A little melted butter
