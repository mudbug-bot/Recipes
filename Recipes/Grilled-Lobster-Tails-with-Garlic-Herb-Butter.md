# Grilled Lobster Tails with Garlic Herb Butter

Serves: 2  
Time: ~25 minutes

## Ingredients

### Lobster Tails
- 2 lobster tails (6–8 oz each)
- Olive oil
- Salt and pepper

### Garlic Herb Butter
- 4 tbsp unsalted butter, melted
- 3 garlic cloves, minced
- 1 tbsp fresh parsley, chopped
- 1 tsp lemon zest
- 1 tbsp fresh lemon juice
- Pinch of smoked paprika
- Salt to taste

## Instructions

1. **Prep the tails** — Using kitchen shears, cut down the center of the top shell lengthwise, stopping at the tail fin. Gently loosen the meat and lift it up so it sits on top of the shell (butterfly style). This helps it cook evenly and soak up the butter.
2. **Make the garlic herb butter** — Mix together the melted butter, garlic, parsley, lemon zest, lemon juice, and paprika. Set aside.
3. **Season** — Brush the lobster meat with olive oil and season with salt and pepper.
4. **Grill** — Preheat your grill to medium-high (around 400°F). Place the tails meat-side down and grill for 5–6 minutes until you get nice grill marks. Flip shell-side down, spoon a generous amount of the garlic herb butter over the meat, and grill another 4–5 minutes until the meat is opaque and reaches an internal temp of 140°F.
5. **Finish and serve** — Remove from the grill, spoon more butter on top, and serve immediately with lemon wedges.

## Tips
- Don't overcook — lobster goes from perfect to rubbery fast. Pull it off the grill the moment it turns opaque.
- Thaw completely if using frozen tails (overnight in the fridge works best).
- Add a pinch of cayenne to the butter if you want a little heat.
- Pairs beautifully with grilled corn, asparagus, or a light pasta.
