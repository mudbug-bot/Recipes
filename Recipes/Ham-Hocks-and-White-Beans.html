Ham Hocks & White Beans

The Luxury of Simplicity

🍲 Serves 4–6 ⏱ 2½–3 hours 💵 ~$10 total

About This Dish

Two of the cheapest ingredients in the grocery store slowly transform into one of the richest, most satisfying bowls of food you can make. Tough smoked pork, humble dried beans, water, and time. The ham hocks simmer, releasing collagen, fat, and deep savory flavor into the broth while the beans soften and release their starch, thickening everything naturally.

After a couple of hours, what started as water and bones becomes something that coats the spoon like gravy. Think of this pot as a foundation — fold in roasted chicken, braised greens, pulled pork, spoon it under grilled sausage or steak, or let it thicken into a sauce.

Ingredients

  • 2 to 3 smoked ham hocks
  • 1 lb dried white beans (Great Northern, navy, or cannellini)
  • 8 cloves garlic, smashed
  • 1 small onion, diced
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 8–10 cups chicken stock
  • 1 tsp smoked paprika
  • 1 tsp black pepper
  • 1 to 2 tbsp rice vinegar
  • Fresh chives, sliced, for garnish

Method

  1. Rinse the beans and pick out any debris. No soaking required.
  2. In a large pot or Dutch oven combine the beans, ham hocks, onion, garlic, bay leaf, paprika, pepper, and chicken stock.
  3. Bring to a simmer and cook gently, partially covered, for about 2½ to 3 hours, stirring occasionally. Add a little water if the beans begin to dry out.
  4. Once the beans are tender and the ham hocks are falling apart, remove the hocks. Pull the meat from the bones, chop it, and return it to the pot.
  5. Stir in the rice vinegar to brighten the broth.
  6. Taste and adjust seasoning if needed. Garnish bowls with fresh chives before serving.

Chef's Trick

Near the end of cooking, crush a small ladle of beans against the side of the pot and stir them back in. The natural starch thickens the broth and gives the whole dish a silky texture without adding anything.
