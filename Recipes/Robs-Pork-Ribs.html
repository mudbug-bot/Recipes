Rob's Pork Ribs

Pellet-grilled ribs with Better Than Bouillon chili base, Bad Byron’s Butt Rub, and a foil wrap with Rob’s BBQ Sauce until tender and nearly fall-off-the-bone.

225°F Pellet Grill 2 cooks + wrap Pull at 180°F+

Ingredients

  • 1 rack pork ribs
  • Better Than Bouillon Chili Base, as binder
  • Bad Byron’s Butt Rub, enough to coat both sides liberally
  • Rob’s BBQ Sauce, generous amount for wrapping

Method

  1. Preheat the pellet grill to 225°F.
  2. Peel the silver skin off the bottom of the ribs.
  3. Apply a thin binding coat of Better Than Bouillon Chili Base.
  4. Season both sides liberally with Bad Byron’s Butt Rub.
  5. Place the ribs on the grill bone-side down and cook for 2 hours.
  6. Remove the ribs from the grill.
  7. Lay out foil the length of the ribs and spread a generous line of Rob’s BBQ Sauce on the foil.
  8. Set the ribs meat-side down on top of the sauce, then squirt more sauce on the bone side.
  9. Wrap tightly in foil and return to the grill meat-side down.
  10. Cook another 1.5 to 2 hours and start checking temperature and tenderness.
  11. Pull when the ribs are at least 180°F and tender to your liking—almost fall off the bone, but not quite.
  12. Rest 15 to 20 minutes before serving.
These are meant to be tender ribs with a little structure left, not mushy fall-apart ribs.
Summary: Smoke low, wrap with Rob’s BBQ Sauce, finish until tender, then give them a short rest before serving.
