# Red Beans & Rice

**Source:** [Arnaud's Restaurant](https://www.arnaudsrestaurant.com/2011/08/recipe-of-the-week-red-beans-rice/)
**Category:** Stews
**Serves:** A crowd

## Notes

This recipe is subject to as many interpretations as there are Creole cooks. Some cooks soak the beans overnight; some do not soak at all. Arnaud's notes that soaking helps reduce the "extra kick" beans can bring for sensitive digestive systems.

Red beans and rice are also served with fried chicken or pork chops.

## Rapid-Soak Method

If you forgot to soak the beans overnight:

1. Cover beans with cold water and bring to a rapid boil.
2. Boil for 5 minutes.
3. Turn off heat, cover, and let stand for 1 hour.
4. Rinse thoroughly with cold water, changing the water two or three times.
5. Proceed with the recipe.

## Ingredients

- 2 pounds (4 cups) dried red beans, soaked overnight or rapid-soak method
- 2 smoked ham hocks
- 1 bottle Abita Amber beer, or other local style beer (optional)
- 1 white or yellow onion, chopped (about 2 cups)
- 1 green pepper, stemmed, seeded, de-ribbed, and chopped
- 2 stalks celery, chopped
- 1/2 cup chopped parsley
- 10 cloves garlic, very finely chopped
- 2 tablespoons Worcestershire sauce
- 1 teaspoon dried oregano
- 2 teaspoons dried thyme
- 4 bay leaves
- 1 3-inch stick cinnamon (optional)
- 1 teaspoon freshly ground black pepper
- 1 teaspoon ground white pepper
- 1/2 teaspoon cayenne pepper
- 2 pieces salt pork, 2 inches each, blanched for 5 minutes in boiling water
- 1 pound smoked sausage, such as Andouille or Kielbasa, cut into 3-inch lengths
- Cooked white rice, for serving
- Water, as needed
- Chicken stock, optional for thinning

## Method

1. In a large stockpot, combine the prepared beans, ham hocks, beer, vegetables, seasonings, salt pork, and sausage.
2. Add 1 gallon of water, making sure the water covers the beans by at least 2 inches.
3. Place over high heat and bring to a boil.
4. Reduce heat and simmer, partially covered, for 3 hours.
5. Remove the cinnamon stick and ham hocks.
6. When the hocks are cool enough to handle, pick off the meat and return it to the pot. Discard the skin and bones.
7. Return to a simmer for about 1 hour more, until the beans begin to break down.
8. If desired, reduce heat very low and simmer another hour, for a total of 5 hours.
9. For best flavor, cool to room temperature, cover, and refrigerate overnight.
10. Remove 1 cup of beans and mash them; return them to the pot.
11. To serve, warm through over low heat, stirring occasionally.
12. If the beans seem too thick, thin with water or chicken stock to the desired consistency.
13. Ladle over white rice.
