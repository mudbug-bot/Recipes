Ras el Hanout Spice Mix

Ras-el-Hanout-Spice-Mix • Makes 1 jar • Spice Blend

A North African medley of fragrant spices you can use in tagines, as a marinade, or anywhere you want warm, layered flavor. Toasting the whole spices first is what makes it pop.

Ingredients

  • 2½ tbsp cumin seeds
  • 2 tbsp coriander seeds
  • 1 tbsp ground cinnamon or 1 cinnamon stick
  • 2 tsp ground ginger
  • 2 tsp black peppercorns
  • 1 tsp ground turmeric
  • ½ tsp cardamom pods (seeds from about 10 pods)
  • A good pinch of saffron (optional)

Method

1. Toast the whole spices

  1. Toast the cumin seeds, coriander seeds, peppercorns, cardamom seeds, and cinnamon stick (if using) in a small dry pan.
  2. Cook just until aromatic and a shade or two darker—don't let them burn.

2. Grind and store

  1. Tip the toasted spices into a spice grinder or mortar.
  2. Add the ground ginger, turmeric, ground cinnamon (if using instead of a stick), and saffron, if using.
  3. Grind to a fine powder.
  4. Store in a sealed jar for up to six months.
Use it in: Lamb tagines, roasted vegetables, marinades for chicken or lamb, spice rubs for grilling, or stirred into soups for a Moroccan edge.
