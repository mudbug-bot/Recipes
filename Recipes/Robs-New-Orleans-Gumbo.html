Ingredients

Roux Pot

  • 1/2 cup bacon fat
  • 4 tbsp butter
  • 2/3 cup all-purpose flour

Brown in Separate Pan (same pan sequence)

  • 3–4 lbs boneless chicken thighs, cut into large chunks
  • 1–2 lbs andouille sausage, diced
  • 2 quarts okra (about 2 lbs), sliced into 1/4-inch rounds
  • Cayenne pepper (for liberal seasoning of chicken)

Build the Gumbo

  • 2 medium onions, sliced
  • 1 medium can tomatoes
  • 2 quarts chicken stock (or water)
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 1 tbsp chopped parsley
  • Freshly ground black pepper

Seafood Finish

  • 1 lb oysters, with oyster liquor (required)
  • 1 lb shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 1 lb crabmeat (optional)

To Serve

  • Boiled rice
  • Gumbo filé (optional)
  • Salt to taste at the table
  • Cayenne, to taste

Method

  1. Make the roux
    In a large heavy gumbo pot, melt bacon fat and butter over medium heat. Whisk in flour and stir constantly, scraping bottom and corners, until the roux reaches a deep dark chestnut brown.
  2. Brown the andouille (separate pan)
    In a large skillet, cook diced andouille just until lightly browned. Remove and hold.
  3. Season and brown the chicken (same pan)
    Season boneless chicken thighs liberally with cayenne pepper (plus black pepper if desired), then brown well in the same skillet. Remove and hold.
  4. Cook okra in the same pan
    In that same skillet (with the rendered drippings), add sliced okra and cook until slightly softened and less slimy, about 5–8 minutes.
  5. Optional: Turkey Stock Upgrade (Highly Preferred)
    Instead of chicken stock, for added complexity create turkey stock from boiled turkey bones, meat, and aromatics. I often make this stock using leftovers from a Thanksgiving turkey dinner — bones, scraps of meat, and pan drippings if available.
    • Turkey bones/meat trimmings: 3–4 lbs
    • Onion (halved): 1
    • Celery stalks (chopped): 2
    • Carrot (chopped): 1
    • Garlic cloves (smashed): 4
    • Bay leaves: 2
    • Black peppercorns: 8–10
    • Water to cover
    Bring to boil, reduce to gentle simmer, cook 2–3 hours, skim as needed, then strain. Discard bones and aromatics and use 2 quarts hot stock in Step 6.
  6. Build the gumbo in the roux pot
    Slowly whisk hot stock into the roux in 2–3 additions, whisking smooth each time before adding more. Then add tomatoes, browned andouille, browned chicken, cooked okra, onions, bay leaf, and parsley. Stir thoroughly to combine.
  7. Simmer
    Bring to a gentle simmer and cook uncovered for 2 hours, stirring occasionally.
  8. Add seafood and remove from heat
    Add oysters with all oyster liquor, then shrimp and crabmeat; stir gently, then remove from heat when seafood is added.
  9. Rest and serve
    Let gumbo rest 15–20 minutes. Serve over hot rice with filé, cayenne, and salt to taste at the table.

Notes

  • Dark roux is the backbone — take it to chestnut, not burnt coffee-black.
  • Adding hot stock gradually keeps the roux base silky and lump-free.
  • Oyster liquor is essential for final depth.
  • Seafood goes in last to protect texture.
  • Even better the next day.
Optional Chef Note: For a final brightness pop, stir in 1–2 tsp fresh lemon juice or mild vinegar just before serving.
