Braised Beef Short Ribs

Make-Ahead-Braised-Beef-Short-Ribs • Serves 6–8 • Beef

This version merges two standout short rib approaches: pre-salting a day or two ahead, low-and-slow braising in red wine and stock, chilling overnight to remove fat, and reducing the braising liquid into a glossy, restaurant-style sauce.

Ingredients

  • 5–6 lbs bone-in beef short ribs (about 12 ribs)
  • Kosher salt and freshly ground black pepper
  • 1 tbsp olive oil (plus more if needed)
  • 1 large yellow onion, large dice
  • 2 large carrots, peeled, large dice
  • 2 ribs celery, large dice
  • 5 large garlic cloves, smashed
  • 2 tbsp tomato paste
  • 2 tbsp fish sauce
  • ⅓ cup tomato purée
  • 1 (750-ml) bottle full-bodied red wine (Cabernet Sauvignon or Zinfandel)
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 5 sprigs fresh thyme
  • 2 sprigs fresh rosemary
  • 4–6 cups veal stock or beef broth (enough to cover meat about ⅔ of the way)
  • Minced fresh herbs (chives or parsley), to finish

Method

1–2 days before: pre-salt the ribs

  1. Season the short ribs generously with kosher salt on all sides.
  2. Place on a tray or in a bowl, cover, and refrigerate 1–2 days. This deeply seasons and improves texture.

Day of braise

2. Temper and brown

  1. Bring ribs out of the fridge about 30 minutes before cooking. Pat dry.
  2. Preheat the oven to 300°F (150°C).
  3. Season with black pepper (and salt now only if you skipped pre-salting).
  4. Heat olive oil in a large Dutch oven over medium-high heat.
  5. Brown the ribs deeply on all sides in batches, 2–3 minutes per side. Do not overcrowd. Set browned ribs aside.

3. Sauté the vegetables

  1. Pour off excess fat, leaving a thin layer.
  2. Add onion, carrots, celery, and garlic.
  3. Cook over medium heat until soft, 5–7 minutes.

4. Build the braising liquid

  1. Add tomato paste, fish sauce, and tomato purée.
  2. Cook for about 1 minute to remove raw tomato flavor.
  3. Pour in the red wine and scrape up all browned bits.
  4. Add bay leaves, thyme, and rosemary.

5. Braise

  1. Return the browned ribs to the pot, nestling them into the liquid.
  2. Add stock until ribs are covered about ⅔ of the way.
  3. Bring to a simmer on the stovetop.
  4. Cover with a lid or parchment cartouche and transfer to the oven.
  5. Braise about 3 hours, until the ribs are fall-off-the-bone tender and a knife slides in and out with little resistance.

Chill overnight

  1. Carefully remove the ribs from the braising liquid and set aside.
  2. Skim any obvious fat from the surface.
  3. Let the pot cool to room temperature, then cover and refrigerate overnight.

Next day: defat, reduce, and finish

  1. Remove and discard the solidified fat from the top.
  2. Reheat the pot gently over low heat until warm.
  3. Strain out vegetables and herbs, or blend everything for a thicker rustic sauce.
  4. Return the strained or blended liquid to the pot and simmer to reduce until thick and glossy.
  5. Taste and adjust seasoning.
  6. Return the short ribs to the sauce to warm through.

Serving & Make-Ahead

Serve the ribs over creamy polenta, mashed potatoes, egg noodles, or risotto, with plenty of sauce spooned over and a scattering of fresh herbs.

Make-ahead: These ribs are even better the next day and freeze well for up to 3 months. Chill in their sauce, then reheat gently on the stovetop.
