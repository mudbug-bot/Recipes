Ingredients

For the salad

  • 8–10 small new potatoes, scrubbed
  • 8 oz (225 g) green beans, trimmed
  • 4 large eggs
  • 2–3 ripe tomatoes, wedges or thick slices
  • 1 small head Boston or Bibb lettuce (or tender mixed greens)
  • 1 small red onion or a few radishes, thinly sliced (optional)
  • 6–8 oz high-quality tuna, preferably in olive oil (canned or jarred)
  • 8–12 Niçoise or other small black olives
  • 6–8 anchovy fillets packed in oil (optional but traditional)
  • 2–3 Tbsp chopped fresh parsley or basil

For the vinaigrette

  • 1 small clove garlic, finely minced
  • 1 Tbsp Dijon mustard
  • 2–3 Tbsp red wine vinegar or lemon juice
  • 6 Tbsp extra-virgin olive oil
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper
  • 1–2 Tbsp chopped fresh herbs (parsley, basil, or chives), optional

Instructions

1. Cook the potatoes

  1. Place the scrubbed new potatoes in a pot of cold, salted water.
  2. Bring to a boil, then reduce to a simmer and cook until just tender when pierced with a knife, 12–15 minutes depending on size.
  3. Drain and let cool slightly. While still warm, slice into halves or thick rounds.
  4. Toss the warm potatoes with a spoonful of vinaigrette and a pinch of salt so they absorb flavor. Set aside.

2. Cook the green beans

  1. Bring a pot of salted water to a boil.
  2. Add the trimmed green beans and cook until just tender-crisp, 3–5 minutes.
  3. Drain and plunge into ice water to stop the cooking and set the color.
  4. Drain well and pat dry. Toss with a little vinaigrette and salt.

3. Cook the eggs

  1. Place eggs in a saucepan and cover with cold water.
  2. Bring just to a boil, then turn off the heat, cover, and let stand 10–12 minutes.
  3. Transfer eggs to cold water to cool, then peel and cut into halves or quarters.
  4. Season lightly with salt and pepper.

4. Make the vinaigrette

  1. In a small bowl, combine the minced garlic, Dijon mustard, and vinegar or lemon juice.
  2. Whisk to combine, then slowly whisk in the olive oil to form an emulsion.
  3. Season with salt, pepper, and chopped herbs if using.
  4. Taste and adjust acidity or seasoning as needed.

5. Prepare the tuna and other components

  1. Drain the tuna and break it into large, attractive chunks.
  2. Lightly spoon some vinaigrette over the tuna to moisten and season.
  3. Slice the tomatoes and season with a little salt, pepper, and a drizzle of vinaigrette.
  4. Wash and dry the lettuce leaves; tear into large pieces.

6. Assemble the salad

  1. On a large platter or individual plates, arrange the lettuce as a base.
  2. Compose the salad in sections: mounds of dressed potatoes, green beans, tomatoes, and tuna.
  3. Arrange the egg halves or quarters around the platter.
  4. Scatter olives and anchovy fillets (if using) over the top.
  5. Drizzle additional vinaigrette over everything or serve it on the side.
  6. Finish with chopped parsley or basil.
Notes: Salade Niçoise should be robust and assertively seasoned, but not soupy with dressing. Each component is seasoned separately, then the whole platter gets a final drizzle. Good tuna, ripe tomatoes, and properly cooked potatoes and beans make all the difference.
