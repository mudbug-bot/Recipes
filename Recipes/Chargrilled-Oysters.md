# New Orleans Chargrilled Oysters (Drago's-Style)

**Serves:** 4 | **Prep:** 10 min | **Cook:** 5–7 min

## The Garlic Butter

- 1 lb (4 sticks) unsalted butter, softened
- 8 cloves garlic, very finely minced
- 1 tbsp fresh oregano, finely chopped (or 1 tsp dried)
- 1 tsp black pepper
- ½ tsp cayenne
- ½ tsp kosher salt

Blend everything together. Make ahead and refrigerate.

## For the Grill

- 24 fresh oysters, on the half shell
- ½ cup grated Parmesan and Romano, mixed
- Leidenheimer's French bread for serving

## Instructions

1. Fire up the grill to screaming hot. Charcoal is best; gas works. You need direct high heat.
2. Place oysters flat-side up on the grate directly over the flame, sitting in their shells holding liquid.
3. Drop a generous tablespoon of garlic butter onto each oyster. It will melt and drip into the fire, causing flare-ups — that's the point.
4. When the butter/oyster liquid begins to bubble vigorously around the edges of the shell (about 2 minutes), they're almost done.
5. Sprinkle the Parm/Romano blend over each oyster. Let it melt and just start to brown, another 30–60 seconds.
6. Pull and serve immediately with French bread for sopping.

## Tips

- Keep oysters deep-side down until heat pops them, or shuck and place on the grate with liquid intact.
- The flare-up from dripping butter chars the oyster slightly at the edges — that's the whole point of "chargrilled."
- More garlic than you think is right. Then add more.
- Drago's uses a butter-to-garlic ratio that would alarm most people. Don't be shy.
