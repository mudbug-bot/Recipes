# Soy-Honey Salmon Candy Bites

Source: Andrew Gruel, Substack – "Soy-Honey Salmon Candy Bites"

A sticky, lacquered salmon bite built by fixing the usual problem: most glazes are watery and slide off fatty fish. Here the glaze is reduced first, cooled so it clings, then the salmon is roasted on a rack and finished with a butter-enriched version of the glaze.

## Serves

3–4 as a main, more as a party snack.

## Glaze Ingredients

- 2 tbsp tamari
- 2–3 tbsp rice vinegar
- 2 tbsp honey
- 2 tbsp Worcestershire sauce

## Salmon

- 1–2 lb boneless, skinless salmon fillet
- Kosher salt

## To Finish

- 2 tbsp butter
- Fresh chives, thinly sliced
- Lime or lemon zest

## Method

1. **Make the glaze.**  
   Add the tamari, rice vinegar, honey, and Worcestershire to a small saucepan. Bring to a simmer and cook until slightly thickened and syrupy—reduced but not as thick as molasses. Remember it will tighten further in the oven. Pour into a bowl and let cool to room temperature (you can speed this up in the fridge).

2. **Prepare the salmon.**  
   Cut the salmon into large cubes, about 1 1/2 inches wide—big enough to stay juicy but small enough to lacquer well. Lightly season with kosher salt and let sit while the glaze cools so the salt can penetrate.

3. **Coat the salmon.**  
   Add a small amount of the cooled glaze to the salmon pieces and gently toss to coat. You want each piece covered, but not swimming in glaze.

4. **Set up the pan.**  
   Line a sheet pan with parchment paper (this is important; the glaze will stick). Place a wire rack on top of the parchment. Arrange the salmon pieces on the rack with space between each piece for air circulation.

5. **Glaze again.**  
   Brush a touch more glaze over each salmon cube.

6. **Roast.**  
   Roast in a 450°F oven for about 8–10 minutes. You’re looking for:
   - Bubbling glaze
   - Browned edges
   - Centers just set but still moist

   Pull the salmon before it overcooks—salmon goes from perfect to dry quickly.

7. **Finish the sauce.**  
   Take the remaining glaze in the bowl. Bring it back to a simmer in the saucepan and whisk in the butter. Swirl until glossy and emulsified into a sauce.

8. **Serve.**  
   Plate the salmon bites, drizzle with the butter-enriched glaze, and finish with fresh chives and citrus zest over the top.

## Notes

- Roasting on a rack over parchment keeps the salmon from sitting in a puddle of burnt sugar and promotes caramelization all around.
- This is ideal as a party bite—if serving guests, consider doubling the recipe; they disappear fast.
- There are no seed oils in this preparation; it relies on tamari, butter, and the natural fat in the salmon.
