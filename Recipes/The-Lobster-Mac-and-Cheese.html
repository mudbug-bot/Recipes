The Lobster Mac & Cheese Everyone Always Asks Me to Make

It's decadent, and it's over-the-top in the best way. It's unapologetically rich; it's also one of the best holiday side dishes you can put on the table.

The key here is restraint where it matters and excess where it counts. This is not a gloppy béchamel-based mac and cheese. It's a cream-forward cheese sauce that stays silky, coats the pasta, and absorbs beautifully as it bakes, without breaking.

⚠️ The Rule: All seafood goes in COOKED.
Lobster: cooked. Crab: cooked. Shrimp: cooked.

Ingredients

Pasta

  • 2 pounds macaroni, cavatappi, or shells
  • Water seasoned aggressively with salt (about 1 tablespoon kosher salt per gallon)

Seafood

  • 2 to 2½ pounds cooked lobster meat, chopped
  • OR 2 pounds cooked crab meat
  • OR 2 pounds cooked shrimp, chopped

Aromatic Base

  • 3 cups dry white wine
  • 6 garlic cloves, smashed
  • 3 sprigs fresh thyme
  • Fresh cracked black pepper

Cream Sauce

  • 2½ to 3 quarts heavy whipping cream
  • Cornstarch slurry (about 3–4 tablespoons cornstarch mixed with cold water)

Cheese Blend

  • 12 oz smoked gouda, shredded
  • 12 oz whole milk mozzarella, freshly shredded
  • 8 oz aged cheddar (white or orange), shredded
  • ½ cup grated Parmesan (plus more for topping)

Topping

  • 1 cup panko breadcrumbs
  • 1 cup crushed potato chips
  • Fresh herbs (parsley or thyme)
  • Extra shredded cheese (optional but encouraged)

Instructions

  1. Cook the pasta: Bring a large pot of water to a rolling boil and salt it generously. Cook the pasta just below al dente (slightly underdone). Drain immediately and shock under cold running water. Set aside.
  2. Reduce aromatics: In a large pot, add white wine, garlic, thyme, and black pepper. Bring to a simmer and reduce until almost dry.
  3. Make the cream sauce: Pour in the heavy cream and return to a gentle simmer. Reduce slightly. Once hot, drizzle in the cornstarch slurry while stirring until the sauce thickens to just past nappe (thicker than coating the back of a spoon, but still fluid). Allow the cornstarch to cook out for a few minutes at a light simmer, constantly stirring.
  4. Add cheese: Remove from heat and strain the sauce, discarding the aromatics. While the cream is still hot, whisk in smoked gouda, mozzarella, and cheddar until smooth, gooey, and fully melted.
  5. Combine: In a very large bowl, add cold pasta and pour over the cheese sauce (use all of it). Fold in your cooked lobster, crab, or shrimp gently. This should look slightly loose and very saucy — that's intentional.
  6. Top and bake: Transfer to a buttered baking dish. Top with panko, crushed potato chips, Parmesan, extra cheese, and fresh herbs. Bake at 375°F for 30–40 minutes, uncovered, until bubbling and golden. Check every 10 minutes at first, then 5 minutes as it gets brown. If you want extra color, broil for the last 2–3 minutes.
Key Tips:
• Don't skimp on sauce
• Don't overcook the pasta
• Don't apologize for how rich it is — that's the point
