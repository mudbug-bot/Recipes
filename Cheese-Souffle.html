Cheese Soufflé

Julia Child • Serves 4

"The soufflé is undoubtedly the egg at its most magnificent." This classic cheese soufflé builds on a thick béchamel base, enriched with egg yolks and lightened with whipped whites, then baked to a dramatic puff.

Ingredients

  • 2 Tb finely grated Parmesan or other hard cheese (for the dish)
  • 2 1/2 Tb butter
  • 3 Tb flour
  • 1 cup hot milk
  • Seasonings: 1/2 tsp paprika, a speck of nutmeg, 1/2 tsp salt, 3 grinds of white pepper
  • 4 egg yolks
  • 5 egg whites (about 2/3 cup)
  • 1 cup (3 1/2 oz) coarsely grated Swiss cheese

Method

Preliminaries

  1. Butter a soufflé dish and roll the grated Parmesan around to coat bottom and sides.
  2. Fasten on a doubled, buttered foil collar rising 3 inches above rim if you want a dramatic puff.
  3. Preheat oven to 400°F with rack in lower third.
  4. Measure out all ingredients.

White sauce (béchamel)

  1. In a saucepan, cook butter and flour together over moderate heat for 2 minutes without coloring.
  2. Remove from heat, let cool a moment, then whisk in all the hot milk.
  3. Return to heat and boil slowly for 3 minutes, stirring; sauce will be very thick.
  4. Whisk in paprika, nutmeg, salt, and white pepper. Remove from heat.

Finish the sauce base

  1. One by one, whisk egg yolks into the hot sauce.

Beat the egg whites

  1. In a clean bowl with clean beaters, beat egg whites to stiff, shining peaks.

Finish the soufflé mixture

  1. Scoop about a quarter of the egg whites onto the sauce and stir in to lighten.
  2. Turn the remaining whites on top.
  3. Rapidly and delicately fold them in, alternating scoops of the spatula with sprinkles of grated Swiss cheese.
  4. Folding the cheese in with the whites keeps the soufflé lighter.
Ahead-of-time: You can hold the mixture for about 30 minutes before baking. Cover loosely with foil and keep away from drafts.

Bake

  1. Set the dish in the preheated 400°F oven and immediately reduce temperature to 375°F.
  2. Bake 25–30 minutes until the soufflé has puffed 2–3 inches over the rim into the collar and the top is nicely browned.

Serve

  1. Remove collar as soon as it’s done.
  2. Bring to the table immediately—soufflés wait for no one.
  3. To serve, hold serving spoon and fork upright and back to back; plunge into the crust and tear it apart.
Doneness checks: The puff should hold when you loosen the collar slightly. For a more set interior, insert a skewer near the side—if it comes out almost clean, it will stand longer; if wet, the center will be creamier but more fragile.
