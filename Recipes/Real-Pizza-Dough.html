Real Pizza Dough (Big Parm)

Real-Pizza-Dough • Makes 2 pies • Bread

The dough from Big Parm—high-gluten flour, a small amount of yeast, and a long ferment. You can do a shorter room-temp rise, but the overnight cold ferment is what gives you New Jersey–style flavor and texture.

Ingredients (about 1,000g dough)

  • 22.1 oz high-gluten flour (about 4½ cups, spooned and leveled)
  • 12.6 oz warm water (about 1½ cups plus 1 tbsp)
  • 0.6 oz kosher salt (about 1 tbsp Diamond Crystal or 2 tsp Morton)
  • 0.04 oz fresh yeast (about a pea-sized piece)
  • If using instant dry yeast: 0.012 oz (about a scant ⅛ tsp)

Method (Mixer)

  1. Add the water and yeast to the mixer bowl. Mix on speed 1 for 1–2 minutes until the yeast dissolves and the water turns slightly cloudy.
  2. Add the flour, then the salt.
  3. Mix on speed 1 until the dough forms a smooth ball and pulls cleanly from the bowl.
  4. Lightly oil a container, add the dough, and cover tightly.
  5. Rest at room temperature for 2–4 hours until nearly doubled.
  6. Divide into 2 dough balls, about 17.6 oz each.
  7. Rest 4–6 hours at room temperature or refrigerate overnight before baking.

Method (By Hand)

  1. In a large bowl, add warm water and yeast; stir until the yeast dissolves.
  2. Add about three-quarters of the flour and mix until a thick batter forms.
  3. Sprinkle in the salt, then add the remaining flour.
  4. Mix until a shaggy dough forms, then turn out onto a work surface.
  5. Knead 8–10 minutes until smooth, elastic, and only slightly tacky.
  6. Form into a tight ball, place in a lightly oiled container, and cover tightly.
  7. Rest 2–4 hours at room temperature until nearly doubled.
  8. Divide into 2 dough balls and rest 4–6 hours at room temperature or refrigerate overnight.

Troubleshooting

  • Too sticky: Lightly dust hands and surface; avoid adding lots of extra flour to the dough.
  • Tight and hard to stretch: Let the dough rest 20–30 minutes; cold dough will fight you.
  • Tears when stretching: Likely under-fermented or under-mixed; longer rest or better knead helps.
  • Spreads too much: Possibly over-proofed; re-ball lightly and rest 15–20 minutes.
  • Pale crust: Preheat the oven and baking surface longer.

Baking

  1. Preheat the oven to its highest setting (500–550°F), with a steel or stone inside.
  2. Preheat for at least 45 minutes.
  3. Position the steel/stone in the lower third of the oven.
  4. Stretch the dough, top quickly, and launch onto the hot surface.
  5. Bake 5–8 minutes, rotating if needed.
  6. Adjust rack position if the bottom burns before the top cooks, or if the pizza is too pale.
Steel vs stone: Steel hits harder and gives more deck-oven energy; stone is gentler and more forgiving in weaker ovens.
