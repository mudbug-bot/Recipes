Soy-Honey Salmon Candy Bites

Soy-Honey-Salmon-Candy-Bites • Seafood • Appetizer or Main

A sticky, lacquered salmon bite built by fixing the usual problem: most glazes are watery and slide off fatty fish. Here the glaze is reduced first, cooled so it clings, then the salmon is roasted on a rack and finished with a butter-enriched version of the glaze.

Serves

3–4 as a main, more as a party snack.

Glaze Ingredients

  • 2 tbsp tamari
  • 2–3 tbsp rice vinegar
  • 2 tbsp honey
  • 2 tbsp Worcestershire sauce

Salmon

  • 1–2 lb boneless, skinless salmon fillet
  • Kosher salt

To Finish

  • 2 tbsp butter
  • Fresh chives, thinly sliced
  • Lime or lemon zest

Method

  1. Make the glaze. Add the tamari, rice vinegar, honey, and Worcestershire to a small saucepan. Bring to a simmer and cook until slightly thickened and syrupy—reduced but not as thick as molasses. It will tighten further in the oven. Pour into a bowl and let cool to room temperature (you can speed this up in the fridge).
  2. Prepare the salmon. Cut the salmon into large cubes, about 1 1/2 inches wide—big enough to stay juicy but small enough to lacquer well. Lightly season with kosher salt and let sit while the glaze cools so the salt can penetrate.
  3. Coat the salmon. Add a small amount of the cooled glaze to the salmon pieces and gently toss to coat. You want each piece covered, but not swimming in glaze.
  4. Set up the pan. Line a sheet pan with parchment paper. Place a wire rack on top of the parchment. Arrange the salmon pieces on the rack with space between each piece for good airflow.
  5. Glaze again. Brush a touch more glaze over each salmon cube.
  6. Roast. Roast in a 450°F oven for about 8–10 minutes. Look for bubbling glaze, browned edges, and centers that are just set but still moist. Pull the salmon before it overcooks.
  7. Finish the sauce. Take the remaining glaze in the bowl, bring it back to a simmer in the saucepan, and whisk in the butter. Swirl until glossy and emulsified into a sauce.
  8. Serve. Plate the salmon bites, drizzle with the butter-enriched glaze, and finish with fresh chives and citrus zest over the top.

Notes

  • Roasting on a rack over parchment keeps the salmon from sitting in burnt sugar and promotes caramelization all around.
  • Great as a main with rice and vegetables, or as a party bite; if serving guests, consider doubling the recipe.
  • No seed oils here—tamari, butter, and the natural fat in the salmon carry the dish.
Summary: Reduce and cool the glaze first so it clings instead of burning on the pan, then finish the remaining glaze with butter for a shiny, restaurant-style sauce.
