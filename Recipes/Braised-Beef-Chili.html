Braised Beef Chili

Serves 3–4 • Slow-braised chuck built on collagen, not shortcuts

This chili leans on collagen and gelatin from long-braised chuck instead of flour or starch. Coffee deepens the beefiness and the chiles' earthy, smoky flavors without making the chili taste like coffee. It’s even better the next day.

Ingredients

Beef

  • 2 lb beef chuck, cut into 1½–2 inch chunks
  • 1¼ tsp kosher salt (plus more to finish)
  • 1/2 tsp freshly cracked black pepper
  • 1–2 Tbsp neutral oil or beef tallow

Base

  • 1½ large yellow onions, diced
  • 4 cloves garlic, smashed
  • 1½ Tbsp tomato paste

Chiles & Spices

  • 2 Tbsp chili powder (ancho or New Mexico preferred; hatch also works)
  • 1½ tsp ground cumin
  • 1½ tsp ground coriander
  • 1½ tsp smoked paprika
  • 3/4 tsp Mexican oregano
  • 3/8 tsp cinnamon (optional)

Liquid

  • 1½ cups beef stock
  • 3/4 cup strong brewed coffee

Finish

  • 1–1½ tsp apple cider vinegar or fresh lime juice
  • Kosher salt, to taste
  • Optional: 1 can kidney or pinto beans, rinsed (add last 30 minutes)

Method

1. Season & sear the beef

  1. Season chuck generously with salt and pepper.
  2. Heat a heavy Dutch oven over medium-high and add oil/tallow.
  3. Sear beef in batches until deeply browned on all sides; remove and set aside. Don’t rush this—this is your flavor base.

2. Onion technique (protect the base)

  1. Lower heat to low. Add onions with a pinch of salt.
  2. Cover and cook gently 8–10 minutes; onions will release moisture and naturally deglaze the fond without burning.
  3. Uncover, raise heat slightly, and cook another 2–3 minutes until soft and translucent (not browned).

3. Tomato paste & garlic

  1. Add garlic and tomato paste.
  2. Cook ~2 minutes until the paste darkens slightly and smells sweet.

4. Bloom spices & deglaze

  1. Add chili powder, cumin, coriander, smoked paprika, oregano, and cinnamon.
  2. Toast ~30 seconds until fragrant.
  3. Deglaze with coffee, scraping the pot clean.

5. Braise

  1. Return beef and any juices to the pot.
  2. Add beef stock until the meat is just barely covered.
  3. Bring to a gentle simmer, cover partially, and cook 2½–3 hours, stirring occasionally, until beef is spoon-tender but still holds its shape.
  4. If using beans, add during the final 30 minutes.

6. Finish & rest

  1. Taste and adjust salt.
  2. Add vinegar or lime juice to brighten.
  3. Remove from heat and let rest 20–30 minutes before serving. It’s even better the next day.

Make It Your Own

Chile profile

  • Ancho / New Mexico → round, mild, earthy
  • Guajillo → brighter, slightly fruity
  • Pasilla → deeper, raisin-like
  • Chipotle → smoke + heat

Swap or blend chiles, keeping total volume similar.

Heat control

  • Fresh jalapeño/serrano with the onions
  • Dried arbol or red pepper flakes for sharper heat
  • Hot sauce or chile oil at the end for adjustable heat

Depth & earthiness

  • Cocoa powder or dark chocolate (¼–½ tsp)
  • Espresso powder (a pinch)
  • Cinnamon, allspice, or clove (use very lightly)

Texture tweaks

  • Mash some beans into the sauce
  • Shred a portion of the beef and fold back in
  • Add a spoon of beef tallow or butter for sheen

Garnishes

  • Raw onion for bite
  • Sour cream or crema
  • Sharp cheddar
  • Fresh herbs

Taste, adjust, and trust time – that’s how the best versions are made.
