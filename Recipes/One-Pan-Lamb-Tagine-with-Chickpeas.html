One-Pan Lamb Tagine with Chickpeas

One-Pan-Lamb-Tagine-with-Chickpeas • Serves 6–8 • Lamb

This oven-baked lamb tagine is built in one pot: ras el hanout–spiced lamb with chickpeas, carrots, harissa, and preserved lemon, finished with yogurt, coriander, and toasted almonds.

Ingredients

  • 1kg lamb neck fillet, cut into large chunks
  • 3 tbsp ras el hanout
  • 2 tbsp olive oil, plus an optional drizzle
  • 2 onions, chopped
  • 4 carrots, cut into large chunks
  • 6 garlic cloves, finely chopped
  • Thumb-sized piece of ginger, peeled and finely grated
  • 2 tbsp rose harissa paste, plus 1 tsp to serve
  • ½ preserved lemon, finely chopped, or a peeled strip of lemon zest
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • 400g can chopped tomatoes
  • 600ml chicken or lamb stock
  • 2 × 400g cans chickpeas, drained but not rinsed
  • 100g dried apricots, roughly chopped (optional)

To serve

  • 150g natural yogurt
  • Small bunch coriander, roughly chopped
  • Small handful flaked almonds, toasted
  • Cooked couscous

Method

1. Season and brown the lamb

  1. Toss the lamb pieces with the ras el hanout and a large pinch of salt. (You can chill this for up to 4 hours.)
  2. Heat the oven to 160C/140C fan/gas 3.
  3. Heat the olive oil in a large flameproof casserole over medium heat.
  4. Brown the lamb on all sides in batches, transferring browned pieces to a plate.

2. Build the base

  1. Cook the onions and carrots in the same pan for 10–12 minutes until soft and golden, adding a drizzle more oil if needed.
  2. Add the garlic and ginger and cook for 2 minutes more.
  3. Stir in the 2 tbsp harissa and preserved lemon, and cook for 1 minute until the vegetables are coated and sticky.
  4. Add the cinnamon stick, then tip in the chopped tomatoes and bring to a simmer.

3. Bake the tagine

  1. Cook the tomato mixture for a few minutes until reduced to a thick paste.
  2. Return the lamb and any resting juices to the pan.
  3. Pour over the stock, season, and bring back to a simmer.
  4. Cover with a lid and transfer to the oven for 1 hour.

4. Add chickpeas and finish

  1. Stir in the chickpeas and dried apricots, if using.
  2. Cover and return to the oven for 1 hour more, until the lamb is tender.
  3. Season to taste, then leave to stand for 10 minutes.

5. Serve

  1. Swirl 1 tsp harissa through the yogurt.
  2. Scatter the tagine with chopped coriander and toasted flaked almonds.
  3. Serve with couscous and the harissa yogurt on the side.
Make-ahead: This tagine keeps well for up to 3 days in the fridge and freezes for 3 months. Reheat gently until piping hot.
