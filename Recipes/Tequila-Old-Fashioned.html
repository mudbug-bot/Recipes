Tequila Old Fashioned

Cocktail • Makes 1

A whiskey classic reimagined with reposado tequila, vanilla syrup, and chocolate bitters. The oak aging on the tequila plays beautifully with the dark, spiced notes — silky, complex, and dangerously easy to drink.

Ingredients

  • 2 oz reposado tequila (the oak aging complements the chocolate and vanilla)
  • ¼ oz vanilla syrup (store-bought or homemade)
  • 2–3 dashes chocolate bitters (Bittermens Xocolatl Mole Bitters are excellent)
  • Large ice cube
  • Orange peel, for garnish
  • Optional: pinch of smoked sea salt

Instructions

  1. Add the tequila, vanilla syrup, and chocolate bitters to a mixing glass filled with ice.
  2. Stir for about 30 seconds until well chilled and properly diluted — stirring rather than shaking keeps it silky and clear.
  3. Strain over a large single ice cube in a rocks glass.
  4. Express an orange peel over the drink by giving it a firm twist to release the oils, then run it around the rim and drop it in (or perch it on the ice).

Tips

  • Reposado is the sweet spot — aged just enough to add depth without overpowering.
  • Añejo will give you something richer and more dessert-like.
  • Avoid blanco — too bright and grassy to pair well with the chocolate and vanilla notes.
  • The smoked salt is optional but genuinely elevates it — a tiny pinch adds subtle complexity that ties everything together.
Key move: Stir, don't shake. Shaking would cloud the drink and dilute it too fast. 30 seconds of stirring gets you the right temperature, dilution, and that beautiful clarity.
