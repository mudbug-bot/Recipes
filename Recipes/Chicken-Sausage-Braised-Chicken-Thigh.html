Chicken Sausage, Braised Chicken Thigh & Red Rice

Braised chicken thighs, smoked sausage, and rice cooked in tomato and stock—this one-pan dinner is built on value cuts and technique. Hard browning, toasted tomato paste, and stock-soaked rice give you a dish that eats like much more than the sum of its parts.

Ingredients (3–5 servings)

  • 1.25 lb boneless chicken thighs
  • 1 lb andouille sausage
  • 1 cup long-grain white rice
  • 1 can cannellini or white beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 small onion, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tbsp tomato paste
  • 2¼ cups chicken stock (hot)
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tsp paprika (smoked if possible)
  • ½ tsp cumin
  • Pinch red pepper flakes
  • Kosher salt and black pepper
  • Optional finish: chopped parsley or a squeeze of lemon

Method

1. Brown the chicken

  1. Pat the chicken thighs dry and season generously with salt and pepper.
  2. Heat the olive oil in a wide, heavy skillet over medium-high heat.
  3. Brown the thighs until deeply golden, about 4–5 minutes per side.
  4. Remove the chicken and set aside.

2. Brown the sausage

  1. In the same pan, add the sliced andouille sausage.
  2. Cook until it takes on good color, then remove and set aside with the chicken.
  3. Do not wipe out the pan—you want the browned bits.

3. Build the red base

  1. Add the diced onion to the pan and cook until soft and lightly caramelised.
  2. Add the minced garlic and the tomato paste.
  3. Cook the tomato paste until it turns dark brick-red and smells sweet, 2–3 minutes.

4. Toast the rice

  1. Stir in the paprika, cumin, and red pepper flakes.
  2. Add the rice and stir until glossy and well coated, about 30–60 seconds.

5. Braise and simmer

  1. Pour in the hot chicken stock, scraping up any browned bits from the bottom of the pan.
  2. Nestle the browned chicken thighs back into the pan.
  3. Scatter the sausage around them.
  4. Cover, bring to a simmer, then reduce heat to low and cook for 18–20 minutes, until the rice is tender.

6. Finish with beans

  1. Stir in the drained beans during the last 5 minutes of cooking, just to warm through and help bind the dish.

7. Rest and serve

  1. Turn off the heat and let the pan rest, covered, for 5 minutes.
  2. Fluff the rice, finish with black pepper and chopped parsley or a squeeze of lemon if you like.
  3. Serve straight from the skillet.
Why it works: The tomato paste, toasted until dark, plus smoked sausage fat and spices, turns the rice into a red, pilaf-style base that tastes way richer than its ingredient list suggests.
