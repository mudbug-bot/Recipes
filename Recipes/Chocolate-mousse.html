Chocolate Mousse

Julia Child • Classic French chocolate mousse

A rich, airy chocolate mousse in the classic French style: melted dark chocolate lightened with whipped egg whites and enriched with a bit of butter and yolks. Best made ahead so it can set and the flavors can develop.

Ingredients

  • 6 oz (170 g) bittersweet chocolate (60–70%), chopped
  • 3 oz (85 g) unsalted butter, cut into pieces and softened
  • 4 large eggs, separated
  • 2/3 cup (130 g) sugar, divided
  • Pinch of salt
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract or 1 Tbsp rum/coffee liqueur (optional)
  • Whipped cream and chocolate shavings, for serving (optional)

Instructions

1. Melt the chocolate

  1. Set up a double boiler: bring a small pot of water to a bare simmer, then turn heat to low.
  2. Place the chopped chocolate in a heatproof bowl over the barely simmering water (the bowl should not touch the water).
  3. Stir gently until the chocolate is just melted and smooth. Remove the bowl from the heat.
  4. Stir in the softened butter until fully incorporated and glossy. Let cool slightly.

2. Prepare the yolk mixture

  1. In a separate bowl, whisk the egg yolks with half of the sugar (about 1/3 cup) until thick, pale, and ribbony.
  2. Optionally, whisk the yolk mixture over a pan of barely simmering water for a minute or two until slightly warm and foamy, then remove and continue whisking off the heat until it cools a bit. (This helps stabilize the mousse.)
  3. Stir the vanilla or liqueur into the yolk mixture, if using.
  4. Fold the yolk mixture into the melted chocolate-butter mixture until smooth.

3. Whip the egg whites

  1. In a clean bowl, add the egg whites with a pinch of salt.
  2. Beat until soft peaks form.
  3. Gradually add the remaining 1/3 cup sugar while whipping, and continue until you have glossy, medium-stiff peaks (not dry).

4. Fold everything together

  1. Stir a small amount of the whipped whites into the chocolate mixture to lighten it.
  2. Gently fold in the remaining whites in 2–3 additions, using a spatula and broad strokes, turning the bowl as you go. Avoid deflating the mixture.
  3. Fold just until no streaks remain and the mousse is uniform and airy.

5. Chill and serve

  1. Spoon or pipe the mousse into serving dishes, ramekins, or a large bowl.
  2. Cover and chill for at least 2–3 hours, or preferably overnight, to set and develop flavor.
  3. Before serving, top with softly whipped cream and chocolate shavings if desired.
Food safety note: This classic version uses raw eggs. If you’re concerned, use pasteurized eggs, or make a yolk mixture warmed to a safe temperature over a water bath and use pasteurized egg whites for whipping.
