Moroccan Roast Lamb

Moroccan-Roast-Lamb • Serves 8 • Lamb

A leg of lamb marinated in ras el hanout, lemon, garlic, and cilantro, then roasted until blackened at the edges and still pink inside. It’s meant to be carved and piled alongside salads, cacık, and flatbreads for building your own sandwiches at the table.

Ingredients

  • 1 leg of lamb, approx. 5½ pounds
  • 1–2 tbsp ras el hanout
  • Juice of 2 lemons
  • 6 tbsp olive oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • Bunch of cilantro, chopped

Method

1. Marinate the lamb

  1. Make incisions all over the leg of lamb with a small knife.
  2. Mix the ras el hanout with the lemon juice, olive oil, minced garlic and chopped cilantro.
  3. Using your fingers, push pinches of the mixture into the incisions.
  4. Rub the remaining paste all over the lamb.
  5. Put the lamb into a large freezer bag, squeeze out any air, tie it, and marinate in the refrigerator overnight or longer.

2. Roast

  1. Heat the oven to 400°F (about 200C).
  2. Take the lamb out of the refrigerator to come to room temperature.
  3. Put the leg of lamb into a roasting pan, scraping any remaining marinade from the bag over the meat.
  4. Roast for about 1½ hours, or until aromatically blackened on the outside and still tender and pink within.
  5. Let the lamb rest for at least 15 minutes (up to an hour) before carving.

3. Serve

  1. Slice the lamb into ragged pieces.
  2. Serve with cacık, lentils, rice, salads, and soft flatbreads or pitas so people can make their own wraps.
Spice note: If you don't have ras el hanout, mix 1 tsp turmeric with 1 tbsp each ground coriander and ground cumin, plus a pinch of ground cinnamon and cloves—it's not traditional, but it gets you into the same aromatic neighborhood.
