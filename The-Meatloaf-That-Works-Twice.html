The Meatloaf That Works Twice

The-Meatloaf-That-Works-Twice • Serves 6–8 • Meat

This is the restaurant-style meatloaf built to slice perfectly, stay juicy, and be even better the next day. It's essentially a giant Italian meatball loaf: panade, cooked aromatics, basil, tomato, and a tight cylinder shape for even cooking.

Ingredients

  • 1 lb ground beef
  • ½–1 lb ground pork
  • 3 slices crustless white bread
  • ½ cup whole milk
  • 1 egg
  • 1 small onion, finely diced
  • 5 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2–3 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil
  • 2 tbsp Worcestershire sauce
  • ½ cup tomato sauce (plus more for glazing)
  • Handful fresh basil, chopped
  • Pinch dried oregano
  • Salt and fresh black pepper
  • Honey, for the glaze

Method

1. Make the panade

  1. In a bowl, mash the bread, milk, and egg into a paste.
  2. Let it sit 5–10 minutes to fully hydrate.

2. Cook the aromatics

  1. Sweat the onion in olive oil with a pinch of salt until soft and slightly sweet.
  2. Add the garlic and cook for about 30 seconds.
  3. Let cool slightly so they don't tighten the meat proteins.

3. Build the base mixture

  1. Add the cooked onion and garlic to the panade.
  2. Stir in basil, oregano, tomato sauce, Worcestershire, salt, and pepper.
  3. Mix until uniform.

4. Add the meat

  1. Fold in the ground beef and pork gently.
  2. Mix until just combined—do not knead like dough.
  3. Let the mixture sit 10–15 minutes so the bread absorbs and proteins relax.

5. Shape the loaf

  1. Lay out two long sheets of plastic wrap.
  2. Form the meat into a log on the wrap.
  3. Roll it tight, like a giant sausage, twisting the ends to compress into a firm cylinder.
  4. Wrap the whole thing in foil.
  5. This gives you even thickness, better cooking, and perfect slices.

6. Bake

  1. Bake the wrapped loaf until the center hits about 155°F.
  2. Rest to let carryover heat finish the cook.

7. Glaze and finish

  1. Warm additional tomato sauce with a knob of butter and a touch of honey.
  2. Unwrap the loaf and transfer to a rack over a tray.
  3. Brush generously with the sauce.
  4. Return to the oven until sticky, shiny, and lightly browned.

Serving Ideas (Round One)

  • Slice thick and serve with extra sauce.
  • Top slices with mozzarella or provolone and melt under the broiler for a knife-and-fork plate.

Round Two: Leftovers

  1. Chill the whole loaf until fully cold.
  2. Slice cold—slices will cut perfectly.
  3. Sear slices in butter until crusty, then cover briefly to steam through.
  4. Serve in hero sandwiches, sliders, or as an easy second-night dinner.
Why it works twice: The panade and tight shaping give you a loaf that behaves more like a large meatball—tender, sliceable, and just as good seared the next day as it is straight from the oven.
