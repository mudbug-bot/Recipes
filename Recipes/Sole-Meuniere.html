Pan-fried sole with brown butter, lemon, and parsley
Delicate fillets of sole lightly floured and sautéed in butter, then finished with a simple sauce of browned butter, lemon, and parsley. This was the dish that famously changed Julia Child’s life when she first tasted it in France.

Ingredients

  • 4 sole fillets (about 5–6 oz / 140–170 g each), skinned and patted dry
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper
  • 1/2 cup all-purpose flour (for dredging)
  • 4–6 Tbsp unsalted butter (divided)
  • 1–2 Tbsp neutral oil (optional, to help prevent burning)
  • 1–2 Tbsp fresh lemon juice (plus lemon wedges for serving)
  • 2–3 Tbsp chopped fresh parsley

Instructions

1. Prepare the fish

  1. Pat the sole fillets very dry with paper towels.
  2. Season both sides lightly with salt and freshly ground black pepper.
  3. Place the flour in a shallow dish and lightly dredge each fillet, shaking off excess flour. You want a thin, even coating.

2. Sauté the sole

  1. In a large skillet (preferably nonstick or well-seasoned), heat 2–3 Tbsp butter over medium heat. Add a tablespoon of oil if you like to help prevent the butter from burning.
  2. When the butter foam subsides and just begins to turn a pale nutty color, add the fillets in a single layer. Do not overcrowd; cook in batches if needed.
  3. Cook the sole 2–3 minutes on the first side, until lightly golden and the fish releases easily from the pan.
  4. Carefully flip the fillets and cook another 1–2 minutes on the second side, until just opaque and cooked through. Thin fillets cook quickly; do not overcook.
  5. Transfer the cooked fillets to a warm platter and tent loosely with foil while you prepare the sauce.

3. Make the meunière butter

  1. Pour off any overly dark butter or burned bits from the pan, but leave a light film of flavorful cooking fat.
  2. Add the remaining 2–3 Tbsp butter to the pan over medium heat.
  3. Cook, swirling the pan, until the butter melts, foams, and turns a light golden brown with a nutty aroma (beurre noisette). Watch closely; it can burn quickly.
  4. As soon as the butter is browned, remove the pan from heat and add the lemon juice (it will sizzle).
  5. Stir in the chopped parsley.
  6. Taste and adjust with a little more lemon or a pinch of salt if needed.

4. Serve

  1. Pour the warm brown butter–lemon–parsley sauce over the plated sole fillets.
  2. Serve immediately with lemon wedges on the side.
  3. Excellent with boiled or sautéed potatoes and a simple green vegetable.
Tip: Have everything ready before you start cooking; the fish and sauce cook very quickly. The key is dry fish, hot pan, and careful attention to the color of the butter.
