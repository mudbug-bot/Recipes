Eggs in the Hole

Eggs-in-the-Hole • Serves 1–2 • Breakfast / Snack

Eggs cooked into the center of butter-toasted bread, finished with Parmesan. It's three ingredients at heart—eggs, bread, butter—but reads like a brunch dish when you treat the details with care.

Ingredients

  • Thick-cut bread (sourdough or country loaf)
  • 2–4 eggs
  • Unsalted butter
  • Salt and freshly cracked black pepper
  • A chunk of Parmesan, for microplaning

Optional

  • Fresh chives or scallions, sliced
  • Hot sauce or jam, on the side

Method

1. Cut the hole (keep the plug)

  1. Use a glass or cutter to punch a clean hole in the center of the bread.
  2. Save the cut-out—it cooks alongside and never goes to waste.

2. Butter the pan and edges

  1. Set a skillet over medium-low heat and melt a generous amount of butter.
  2. Don't let the butter brown; just let it foam.
  3. Lay the bread slice and the cut-out into the pan so they slowly absorb the butter.
  4. Brush melted butter along the inside edge of the cut-out; that inner rim needs fat to fry, not steam.
  5. Flip the bread so the toasted side is up before adding the egg.

3. Add the egg and season

  1. Crack an egg directly into the center hole.
  2. Season immediately with salt and freshly cracked black pepper.
  3. Lower the heat.

4. Steam, don't flip

  1. Add a small dash of water to the pan—enough to create steam, not a puddle.
  2. Cover the pan briefly so the whites set from gentle steam while the yolk stays soft.
  3. Lift the lid as needed so the bread doesn't steam and go soggy; you're balancing steam and crispness.

5. Finish in fresh butter

  1. Once the whites are set to your liking, uncover the pan.
  2. Add a small knob of fresh butter and let the bread finish toasting in the fat.

6. Shower with Parmesan

  1. Turn off the heat.
  2. While everything is still hot, microplane Parmesan generously over the top.
  3. The cheese should melt instantly and cling to the egg and bread.
Key points: Steam sets the egg without overcooking the yolk, buttered edges keep the bread crisp, and finishing in fresh butter plus Parmesan is what makes it feel restaurant-level, even though it's pantry food.
