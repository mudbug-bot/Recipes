## Why This Recipe

This merged recipe combines the best techniques from two exceptional short ribs recipes:
- **Pre-salting 1-2 days ahead** ensures perfect seasoning throughout (Kendall)
- **Overnight chilling** removes excess fat for a rich, not greasy sauce (Simply)
- **Lower, slower cooking** at 300°F for 3 hours = fall-off-the-bone tenderness (Kendall)
- **Umami boosters** (fish sauce + tomato paste) add incredible depth (Kendall)
- **Generous wine** (full bottle) creates a luxurious braising liquid (Simply)
- **Final reduction** creates a thick, glossy glaze (Simply)

## The Keys to Great Short Ribs

1. **Pre-salt 1-2 days ahead** — Even a few hours helps, but overnight is best. The salt permeates the meat for perfect seasoning.
2. **Brown well** — Deep golden crust = deep flavor. Don't overcrowd the pot.
3. **Low and slow** — 300°F for ~3 hours breaks down connective tissue without drying.
4. **Chill overnight** — Makes fat removal easy and lets flavors meld.
5. **Reduce to glaze** — The final reduction concentrates flavor and creates that restaurant-quality shine.

## Other Tips

- **Use good wine** — Use a wine you'd actually drink. Cabernet Sauvignon or Zinfandel work great.
- **Use veal stock if possible** — Find it at your local butcher. Beef stock works too.
- **Don't skip the fish sauce** — It won't taste fishy, just adds umami depth.
- **Cartouche vs. lid** — A parchment paper lid (cartouche) allows slight evaporation while keeping the braise moist. Search YouTube for how to make one.

## Ingredients

- 5-6 lbs bone-in beef short ribs (about 12 ribs)
- Kosher salt and freshly ground black pepper
- 1 Tbsp olive oil (plus more if needed)
- 1 large yellow onion, cut into large dice
- 2 large carrots, peeled and cut into large dice
- 2 ribs celery, cut into large dice
- 5 large garlic cloves, smashed and left whole
- 2 Tbsp tomato paste
- 2 Tbsp fish sauce*
- ⅓ cup tomato puree
- 1 (750-ml) bottle full-bodied red wine (Cabernet Sauvignon or Zinfandel)
- 2 bay leaves
- 5 sprigs fresh thyme
- 2 sprigs fresh rosemary
- 4-6 cups veal stock or beef broth (enough to cover meat ⅔ of the way)
- Minced fresh herbs to finish (chives or parsley)

## Instructions

### 1-2 Days Before (Optional but Recommended)

**Pre-salt the ribs:**  
Season the short ribs on all sides with kosher salt. Place in a large bowl or on a sheet tray and cover tightly with plastic wrap. Refrigerate for 1-2 days. This step is the difference between great and fantastic short ribs!

### Day of Dinner

1. **Bring ribs to room temperature:**  
   Remove short ribs from refrigerator about 30 minutes before cooking. Pat them very dry with paper towels. Season generously with freshly ground black pepper. (If you skipped pre-salting, season with kosher salt now too.)

2. **Preheat oven to 300°F.**

3. **Brown the short ribs:**  
   In a large Dutch oven over medium-high heat, add olive oil. Brown short ribs on all sides, 2-3 minutes per side, until deep golden brown. Work in batches—don't overcrowd (this inhibits browning). Set browned ribs aside and discard excess fat from pot.

4. **Sauté the vegetables:**  
   Lower heat to medium. Add onion, carrots, celery, and garlic to the same pot. Cook until vegetables are soft, 5-7 minutes, stirring occasionally.

5. **Build the braising liquid:**  
   Add tomato paste, fish sauce, and tomato puree. Cook for 1 minute until raw tomato flavor cooks off. Add wine and scrape up all the browned bits from the bottom of the pot.

6. **Add herbs and liquid:**  
   Add bay leaves, thyme, and rosemary. Stir to combine. Nestle the browned short ribs into the mixture. Add beef broth/stock until liquid covers the meat about ⅔ of the way up.

7. **Braise in oven:**  
   Bring to a simmer over medium heat. Cover with a cartouche (parchment lid) or regular lid. Transfer to oven and cook for ~3 hours, until short ribs are fall-off-the-bone tender and a knife inserts/pulls out with no tension.

8. **Cool and chill overnight:**  
   Remove short ribs from pot and set aside (bones may be falling off—discard loose ones). Skim off surface fat. Allow mixture to cool to room temperature in the pot, then cover tightly with plastic wrap and lid. Refrigerate overnight.

### Next Day

9. **Remove solidified fat:**  
   Remove pot from refrigerator. The fat will have solidified on top—scoop it all off and discard. This is much easier when cold!

10. **Reheat and reduce:**  
    Transfer pot to stove over low heat. Bring up to temperature slowly with lid on. Simmer for 20-30 minutes until ribs are warmed through. (Test by inserting a paring knife into the center of the biggest rib for 10 seconds—touch to your inner wrist; if warm, it's ready.)

11. **Finish the sauce:**  
    Remove short ribs and set aside. Strain the sauce and discard vegetables/herbs, OR puree everything in a high-powered blender for a rustic, thicker sauce. Return sauce to pot and simmer over medium-low heat until reduced to your desired thickness. Taste and adjust seasoning with salt if needed.

12. **Serve:**  
    Return short ribs to the sauce to warm through, or plate ribs and spoon sauce over top. Serve over creamy polenta, mashed potatoes, or egg noodles. Finish with minced fresh herbs.

## Make-Ahead & Storage

**Refrigerator:** Store in an airtight container for 3-4 days. Reheat over medium-low heat on stovetop in a covered pot, stirring frequently.

**Freezer:** Freeze with sauce in an airtight, freezer-safe container (or freezer bag with air removed) for up to 3 months. Defrost in refrigerator and reheat on stovetop.

**Make-ahead tip:** This dish tastes even better the next day as flavors continue to meld. Perfect for dinner parties!

## Notes

**Fish Sauce:** This umami-forward ingredient adds depth you can't quite pinpoint. Similar to tomato paste, it transforms an ordinary braise into something extra-special. The dish will NOT taste like fish—promise!

**Cartouche (Parchment Lid):** Allows slight evaporation while keeping the braise moist. Cut a circle of parchment paper to fit your pot, then cut a small hole in the center for steam to escape. Many YouTube tutorials show how to make one.

**Sauce Options:**
- **Strained** — More refined presentation, smoother sauce
- **Pureed** — Rustic, thicker sauce with all the vegetable flavor

**Non-Alcoholic Substitute:** Replace wine with additional stock spiked with balsamic vinegar and a dab of tomato paste. Won't be identical, but still delicious.

**Oven Temperature:** 300°F is lower than many recipes (350°F+) but produces more tender results. Trust the process!
