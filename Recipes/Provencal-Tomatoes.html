Ingredients

  • 6 medium ripe tomatoes
  • 1–2 cloves garlic, very finely minced
  • 1/3–1/2 cup fresh breadcrumbs
  • 2–3 Tbsp chopped fresh parsley
  • 1–2 Tbsp chopped fresh herbs (thyme, oregano, or basil), or 1 tsp dried Herbes de Provence
  • 3–4 Tbsp olive oil (plus more for drizzling)
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper

Instructions

1. Prepare the tomatoes

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Lightly oil or butter a shallow baking dish.
  3. Cut the tomatoes in half crosswise (through the equator).
  4. Gently squeeze or scoop out some of the seeds and watery pulp, being careful to leave enough flesh so the tomato halves hold their shape.
  5. Sprinkle the cut sides lightly with salt and place them cut side down on a rack or paper towel for 10–15 minutes to drain excess moisture.

2. Make the breadcrumb topping

  1. In a bowl, combine the fresh breadcrumbs, minced garlic, chopped parsley, and other herbs.
  2. Season with a little salt and freshly ground black pepper.
  3. Add 3–4 Tbsp olive oil and mix until the crumbs are evenly moistened but not greasy. The mixture should be loose and fluffy, not a paste.

3. Assemble

  1. Place the drained tomato halves cut side up in the prepared baking dish.
  2. Spoon the breadcrumb-herb mixture onto each tomato half, pressing lightly so it stays in place.
  3. Drizzle a little olive oil over the tops.

4. Bake

  1. Bake in the preheated oven for 20–30 minutes, until the tomatoes are tender and the topping is lightly browned and crisp.
  2. If the tops need more color, you can finish them under the broiler for a minute or two—watch carefully to avoid burning.

5. Serve

  1. Serve hot or warm as a side dish with grilled or roasted meats, poultry, or fish.
  2. Garnish with a little extra chopped parsley if you like.
Variation: Add a spoonful of finely grated Parmesan or Gruyère cheese to the breadcrumb mixture for a richer gratin topping.
