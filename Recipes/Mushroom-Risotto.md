# Mushroom Risotto

A rich mushroom risotto finished with cream, Parmesan, parsley, thyme, and black pepper. This recipe is also used as the base for **Skewered Scallops over Mushroom Risotto**.

## Yield
6–8 servings

## Ingredients
- 5 Tbl unsalted butter
- 3 Tbl minced shallots
- 2 cups Arborio rice
- 4–6 cups chicken stock, heated
- 1 Tbl kosher salt, added to the chicken stock
- 3/4 lb wild mushrooms (shiitakes, porcinis, morels, chanterelles, creminis, portobellos, oysters), cleaned and sliced
- 1 cup heavy cream
- 1/2 cup freshly grated Parmesan cheese
- 2 Tbl chopped fresh parsley
- 1 tsp chopped fresh thyme
- 1 tsp freshly ground black pepper

## Instructions
1. In a very large skillet, heat 3 tablespoons of butter over medium heat and add the shallots. Cook until the shallots become soft.
2. Add the rice. Stir constantly to prevent the rice from browning. The grains of rice need to get hot.
3. Add 1 1/2 cups of hot stock and lower the heat until the stock is barely simmering. Continue to stir constantly.
4. As the stock is absorbed, add more stock in small amounts. Continue this process until the rice has become slightly tender.
5. In a separate skillet, place the remaining 2 tablespoons of butter over medium heat. Add the mushrooms and sauté until soft.
6. Add the mushrooms to the risotto.
7. When the rice is almost completely cooked, add the cream and stir slowly until absorbed.
8. Remove from heat and stir in the cheese, fresh herbs, and pepper.
9. Serve immediately.
