Coquilles Saint-Jacques

Julia Child • Scallops in white wine and cream, gratinéed with cheese

Tender scallops poached in white wine, folded into a rich mushroom and shallot sauce, then topped with cheese and browned under the broiler. A classic French first course served in scallop shells or gratin dishes.
📝 Shopping List

Ingredients

For the scallops

  • 1 lb sea scallops, rinsed and patted dry
  • 1 cup dry white wine
  • 1/2 cup fish or clam stock (or additional wine)
  • 1 small carrot, sliced
  • 1 small onion, sliced
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 2–3 parsley sprigs
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper

For the sauce and gratin

  • 3 Tbsp butter (divided)
  • 1 Tbsp olive oil (optional, for mushrooms)
  • 8 oz mushrooms, sliced
  • 1–2 shallots, finely minced
  • 2 Tbsp flour
  • 3/4–1 cup reserved scallop poaching liquid (wine/stock mixture)
  • 1/2–3/4 cup heavy cream
  • 1–2 Tbsp grated Parmesan or Gruyère cheese (for sauce)
  • 1/2–3/4 cup grated Gruyère and/or Swiss cheese (for topping)
  • 2–3 Tbsp fresh breadcrumbs
  • 2 Tbsp chopped fresh parsley (for garnish)

Instructions

1. Poach the scallops

  1. In a wide saucepan, add the wine, stock, carrot, onion, bay leaf, and parsley sprigs. Season lightly with salt and pepper.
  2. Bring to a gentle simmer and cook for 10 minutes to flavor the liquid.
  3. Add the scallops in a single layer if possible.
  4. Simmer very gently for 3–5 minutes, just until the scallops are barely firm and opaque. Do not overcook.
  5. Remove the scallops with a slotted spoon and set aside in a bowl.
  6. Strain the poaching liquid and reserve 3/4–1 cup for the sauce.

2. Prepare the mushrooms

  1. In a skillet, melt 1 Tbsp butter with 1 Tbsp olive oil over medium-high heat.
  2. Add the sliced mushrooms and sauté until they release their liquid and then begin to brown.
  3. Season lightly with salt and pepper. Set aside.

3. Make the sauce

  1. In a saucepan, melt 2 Tbsp butter over medium heat.
  2. Add the minced shallots and cook until softened but not browned.
  3. Stir in the flour and cook 1–2 minutes to form a roux.
  4. Gradually whisk in the reserved poaching liquid, stirring until smooth and slightly thickened.
  5. Add the cream and simmer gently for a few minutes until the sauce coats the back of a spoon.
  6. Stir in 1–2 Tbsp grated Parmesan or Gruyère. Taste and adjust seasoning with salt and pepper.

4. Combine scallops and sauce

  1. Fold the scallops and sautéed mushrooms into the sauce, coating them gently.
  2. If the sauce seems too thick, thin with a bit more cream or poaching liquid. If too thin, simmer briefly to reduce.

5. Assemble in shells or gratin dishes

  1. Preheat the broiler.
  2. Butter individual scallop shells or small gratin dishes.
  3. Spoon the scallop mixture into the dishes, dividing evenly.
  4. Top each with a sprinkling of fresh breadcrumbs and a generous layer of grated Gruyère/Swiss cheese.

6. Gratinée and serve

  1. Place the dishes under the broiler, a few inches from the heat, and broil just until the tops are bubbly and lightly browned.
  2. Watch closely to avoid burning.
  3. Remove carefully (dishes will be very hot) and sprinkle with chopped fresh parsley.
  4. Serve immediately while sizzling, with crusty bread or as a first course.
Make ahead: You can poach the scallops and make the sauce a few hours in advance. Combine and assemble in shells, then refrigerate. Bring to room temperature, top with cheese and breadcrumbs, and gratinée just before serving.
