Herb-Crusted Ras El Hanout Lamb Chops

Servings: 4 | Prep: 15 minutes | Cook: 10 minutes

Gather around, because these Herb-Crusted Ras El Hanout Lamb Chops are about to become your new favorite weeknight dinner. You'll love how the warm, aromatic spices pair with the fresh herbs and tender meat. It's impressive enough for guests but simple enough for a cozy night in.
📝 Shopping List

Ingredients

  • Lamb chops – 8 pieces
  • Ras el hanout – 2 tbsp
  • Fresh rosemary – 2 tbsp, finely chopped
  • Fresh thyme – 1 tbsp, finely chopped
  • Garlic – 3 cloves, minced
  • Olive oil – 2 tbsp
  • Salt – 1 tsp
  • Black pepper – ½ tsp

Instructions

  1. Pat the lamb chops dry with paper towels to help the crust stick better.
  2. In a small bowl, combine the ras el hanout, rosemary, thyme, garlic, salt, and black pepper.
  3. Rub the olive oil evenly over all sides of each lamb chop.
  4. Press the spice and herb mixture firmly onto both sides of each chop, coating them completely.
  5. Heat a large skillet over medium-high heat until a drop of water sizzles immediately.
  6. Place the lamb chops in the skillet without overcrowding; cook in batches if needed.
  7. Sear the chops for 3 minutes on the first side until a golden-brown crust forms.
  8. Flip the chops and cook for another 3 minutes for medium-rare, or until they reach 145°F on a meat thermometer.
  9. Transfer the chops to a plate and let them rest for 5 minutes to allow the juices to redistribute.
  10. Serve immediately while warm. A quick tip: don't move the chops while searing to get that perfect crust!

Notes

As you take your first bite, you'll notice the crispy herb crust gives way to juicy, flavorful lamb with a hint of warmth from the ras el hanout. Try serving these over a bed of couscous or with a simple yogurt sauce to balance the spices. Leftovers make a fantastic addition to salads the next day—just slice them thin and toss them in.
