Bouillabaisse

Julia Child • Serves 6–8 • Soup

Bouillabaisse is one of the most debated dishes in France – every coastal village has its own version. Julia adapts the classic Marseille fisherman’s stew for cooks who don’t live on the Mediterranean, focusing on a mix of firm and tender fish and optional shellfish.
On fish selection (Julia): Ideally you should pick six or more varieties of fresh fish. Some should be firm-fleshed and gelatinous (halibut, eel, winter flounder) and some tender and flaky (hake, baby cod, small pollock, lemon sole). Shellfish are optional but add color and glamor.

Ingredients

Aromatics & Base

  • 1 cup minced onions
  • 3/4 cup minced leek (or 1/2 cup additional onions)
  • 1/2 cup olive oil
  • 4 cloves mashed garlic
  • 1 lb ripe tomatoes, roughly chopped or 1 1/2 cups drained canned tomatoes or 1/2 cup tomato paste
  • 2 1/2 quarts water

Herbs & Seasoning

  • 6 parsley sprigs
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 1/2 tsp thyme or basil
  • 1/2 tsp fennel
  • 2 big pinches saffron
  • 2-inch piece or 1/2 tsp dried orange peel
  • 1/2 tsp pepper
  • 1 Tb salt (omit if using clam juice)

Stock & Fish

  • 3–4 lb fish heads, bones, and trimmings or 1 quart clam juice + 1 1/2 quarts water (no salt)
  • 6–8 lb assorted lean fish, and shellfish if you wish

Suggested Fish (Julia’s ideas)

  • Rock, calico or sea bass; cod or lingcod; flounder; grouper; haddock; hake or whiting; halibut; lemon sole; perch; pollock; porgy; redfish; scrod; snapper; trout; plus clams, scallops, mussels, crab, or lobster if desired.

Method

1. Start the base

  1. Cook the onions and leeks slowly in olive oil for about 5 minutes until almost tender but not browned.
  2. Stir in the garlic and tomatoes. Raise heat to moderate and cook 5 minutes more.

2. Build the broth

  1. Add the water, herbs, seasonings, and fish heads/bones/trimmings (or clam juice + water) to the kettle.
  2. Cook uncovered at a moderate boil for 30–40 minutes.
  3. Strain the soup into a clean saucepan, pressing juices out of solids. Discard solids.
  4. Adjust seasoning and add more saffron if needed. You should have about 2 1/2 quarts.

3. Cook the fish

  1. Transfer the strained broth to a higher, rather narrow kettle.
  2. 20 minutes before serving, bring the soup to a rapid boil.
  3. Add lobsters, crabs, and firm-fleshed fish first. Return to a boil and cook rapidly for 5 minutes.
  4. Add tender-fleshed fish, clams, mussels, and scallops. Bring back to a rapid boil and cook 5 minutes more, just until fish are tender when pierced with a fork. Do not overcook.

4. Serve

  1. Lift out the fish and arrange on a heated platter.
  2. Pour the soup into a tureen over rounds of French bread.
  3. Spoon a ladleful of soup over the fish on the platter.
  4. Sprinkle parsley over both fish and soup and serve immediately, optionally with rouille.

Prep note: Have fish cleaned and scaled. Discard gills, save heads and trimmings for stock, cut large fish into 2-inch crosswise slices, scrub clams and mussels, clean scallops, and split live crab/lobster just before cooking.
