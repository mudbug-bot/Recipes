Classic French chicken braised in red wine
Coq au Vin is rustic French cooking at its best: chicken slowly braised in red wine with bacon, mushrooms, onions, and aromatics until tender and deeply flavored. Serve with potatoes, noodles, or crusty bread to soak up the sauce.

Ingredients

For the chicken

  • 1 (3–4 lb) chicken, cut into serving pieces (or 8 bone-in thighs/drumsticks)
  • 4–6 oz slab bacon or thick-cut bacon, cut into lardons
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper
  • 2–3 Tbsp butter or oil (as needed)
  • 1 carrot, sliced
  • 1 onion, sliced
  • 2 cloves garlic, smashed
  • 1–2 Tbsp flour
  • 2–3 cups red wine (Burgundy, Côtes du Rhône, or similar)
  • 1–2 cups chicken stock (enough to almost cover the chicken)
  • 1 Tbsp tomato paste
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 1/2 tsp dried thyme
  • 2 Tbsp chopped fresh parsley (for garnish)

For the garnish

  • 18–24 small pearl onions, peeled
  • 8 oz mushrooms, quartered
  • 2 Tbsp butter
  • 1 Tbsp oil
  • Salt and pepper

Instructions

1. Prepare the bacon and chicken

  1. Blanch the bacon lardons in simmering water for 5 minutes, then drain and pat dry.
  2. In a heavy Dutch oven, sauté the bacon over medium heat until lightly browned. Remove with a slotted spoon and set aside, leaving the fat in the pan.
  3. Pat the chicken pieces dry and season with salt and pepper.
  4. Brown the chicken in batches in the bacon fat (add butter or oil if needed) until well colored on all sides. Remove and set aside with the bacon.

2. Build the braising base

  1. In the same pot, add the sliced carrot and onion. Cook until lightly browned and softened.
  2. Add the smashed garlic and cook briefly until fragrant.
  3. Sprinkle the flour over the vegetables and cook 1–2 minutes, stirring, to form a light roux.
  4. Stir in the tomato paste.
  5. Pour in the red wine, scraping up any browned bits from the bottom of the pot.
  6. Add enough chicken stock to almost cover the chicken when it goes back in.
  7. Add the bay leaf, thyme, and the browned bacon.
  8. Return the chicken pieces (and any juices) to the pot.
  9. Bring to a gentle simmer.

3. Braise the chicken

  1. Cover the pot and simmer very gently on the stovetop, or transfer to a 300°F (150°C) oven.
  2. Braise for 45–60 minutes, until the chicken is tender and cooked through but not falling apart. Adjust heat as needed to maintain a lazy simmer.

4. Prepare the garnish

Pearl onions

  1. In a skillet, heat 1 Tbsp butter and 1 Tbsp oil over medium heat.
  2. Add the peeled pearl onions and sauté, rolling them gently, until lightly browned.
  3. Add a splash of stock or water, cover, and cook until the onions are tender and the liquid has mostly evaporated.
  4. Season with salt and pepper.

Mushrooms

  1. In another pan, sauté the mushrooms in 1 Tbsp butter over medium-high heat until they release their liquid and then begin to brown.
  2. Season with salt and pepper.

5. Finish the sauce

  1. When the chicken is done, remove the pieces to a warm platter along with the bacon.
  2. Skim excess fat from the surface of the braising liquid.
  3. If needed, boil the sauce for a few minutes to reduce to a light coating consistency. If too thick, thin with a bit of stock or wine.
  4. Taste and adjust seasoning with salt and pepper.
  5. Add the cooked onions and mushrooms to the sauce, or arrange them around the chicken on the platter and spoon some sauce over.

6. Serve

  1. Pour some of the sauce and vegetables over the chicken, and serve the rest in a sauce boat.
  2. Sprinkle with chopped parsley.
  3. Serve with buttered potatoes, noodles, or crusty bread to soak up the sauce.
