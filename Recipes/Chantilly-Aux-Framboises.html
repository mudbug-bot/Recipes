Chantilly Aux Framboises

A favorite from Rob's recipe collection.

Dessert

Ingredients

  • 1 1/2 pints fresh raspberries
  • 2/3 cup berry sugar (very fine/caster sugar)
  • 8 egg yolks
  • 2 1/2 cups chilled heavy whipping cream
  • Optional: ladyfingers, crushed, for the bottom of dessert glasses

Method

1. Raspberry purée

  1. Wash and drain raspberries.
  2. Force them through a sieve into a bowl.
  3. Measure out 1 1/4 cups of purée and chill.

2. Egg yolk ribbon

  1. Beat sugar into egg yolks until pale yellow and the mixture falls back on itself in a slowly dissolving ribbon.
  2. Set the bowl over not-quite-simmering water and beat until thickened and uncomfortably hot to the touch.
  3. Set bowl in ice water and beat until cold and again forming a slowly dissolving ribbon. Fold with a spatula until thoroughly chilled.

3. Whip the cream

  1. Beat chilled heavy cream until doubled in volume and forming stiff (not soft) peaks.

4. Combine

  1. Fold the chilled raspberry purée into the chilled egg yolk mixture.
  2. Fold in the whipped cream until fully combined and airy.

5. Assemble & chill

  1. Optionally crush ladyfingers and spoon crumbs into the bottom of dessert glasses.
  2. Fill bowl or glasses with the raspberry cream.
  3. Cover and refrigerate at least 6 hours or overnight.

6. Serve

  1. Decorate the top with fresh raspberries.
  2. Optionally add a dollop of whipped cream on each portion.

Note: It takes just under four small 6 oz clam-shells of raspberries to yield about 1 1/4 cups of purée.
