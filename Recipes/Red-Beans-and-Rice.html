Red Beans & Rice

A classic Creole pot of red beans with ham hocks, sausage, aromatics, herbs, and spice — slow-simmered until rich and creamy, then ladled over white rice.

🍲 Serves a crowd ⏱ 4–5 hours 🫘 Classic Creole
This recipe is subject to as many interpretations as there are Creole cooks. Some soak beans overnight, some do not. Arnaud's version leans into a long, gentle simmer and tastes even better the next day.

Rapid-Soak Method

  1. Cover the beans with cold water and bring to a rapid boil.
  2. Boil for 5 minutes.
  3. Turn off the heat, cover, and let stand for 1 hour.
  4. Rinse thoroughly with cold water, changing the water two or three times.
  5. Proceed with the recipe.

Ingredients

  • 2 pounds dried red beans, soaked overnight or rapid-soak method
  • 2 smoked ham hocks
  • 1 bottle Abita Amber beer or similar local-style beer (optional)
  • 1 white or yellow onion, chopped
  • 1 green pepper, chopped
  • 2 stalks celery, chopped
  • 1/2 cup chopped parsley
  • 10 cloves garlic, finely chopped
  • 2 tablespoons Worcestershire sauce
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 2 teaspoons dried thyme
  • 4 bay leaves
  • 1 cinnamon stick, 3 inches (optional)
  • 1 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1 teaspoon white pepper
  • 1/2 teaspoon cayenne
  • 2 pieces salt pork, blanched
  • 1 pound smoked sausage, such as Andouille or Kielbasa
  • Cooked white rice, for serving

Method

  1. In a large stockpot, combine the beans, ham hocks, beer, vegetables, seasonings, salt pork, and sausage.
  2. Add 1 gallon of water, making sure the beans are covered by at least 2 inches.
  3. Bring to a boil over high heat.
  4. Reduce heat and simmer, partially covered, for 3 hours.
  5. Remove the cinnamon stick and ham hocks.
  6. Pick the meat from the hocks and return it to the pot. Discard skin and bones.
  7. Simmer about 1 hour more, until the beans begin to break down.
  8. If desired, simmer another hour on very low heat for a total of 5 hours.
  9. For best flavor, cool, cover, and refrigerate overnight.
  10. Remove 1 cup of beans, mash them, and stir them back in.
  11. Warm gently. If too thick, thin with water or chicken stock.
  12. Ladle over white rice.

Serving Notes

Arnaud's notes that red beans and rice are also great served with fried chicken or pork chops.

Best flavor move: make it a day ahead, chill overnight, then reheat and serve.
Summary: A long-simmered New Orleans classic with ham hocks, sausage, herbs, and red beans — humble ingredients, big payoff.
