Simple French roast chicken with pan juices
A whole chicken roasted simply and correctly: well-dried, generously seasoned, and basted as it cooks. The result is crisp skin, juicy meat, and a pan full of flavorful juices to spoon over the carved bird.

Ingredients

  • 1 whole chicken (3½–4½ lb / 1.6–2 kg)
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper
  • 2–3 Tbsp softened butter
  • 1 small onion, halved
  • 1 small carrot, cut into chunks
  • 1 small celery stalk, cut into chunks
  • 2–3 garlic cloves, lightly crushed (optional)
  • 1–2 sprigs fresh thyme or tarragon (optional)
  • 1–2 Tbsp olive oil or additional butter for basting
  • 1/2–1 cup chicken stock or water (for deglazing, as needed)

Instructions

1. Prepare the chicken

  1. Preheat the oven to 425°F (220°C).
  2. Remove any giblets from the cavity; reserve for stock if desired.
  3. Pat the chicken very dry inside and out with paper towels. Dryness helps crisp the skin.
  4. Season the cavity generously with salt and pepper.
  5. Place the onion, carrot, celery, garlic, and herbs (if using) inside the cavity.

2. Truss and season the bird

  1. Truss the chicken with kitchen twine: tie the legs together and tuck the wing tips under the body. This helps it cook evenly.
  2. Rub the outside of the chicken with softened butter.
  3. Season the skin all over with salt and freshly ground black pepper.

3. Start roasting

  1. Place the chicken breast-side up on a rack in a roasting pan, or on a bed of sliced onions and carrots in the pan.
  2. Roast at 425°F (220°C) for 15–20 minutes to start browning the skin.
  3. Baste the chicken quickly with a little melted butter or its own fat.

4. Continue roasting at lower temperature

  1. Reduce the oven temperature to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Continue roasting, basting every 15–20 minutes with the pan juices (tilting the pan to collect them) or with a bit more butter/oil if needed.
  3. Roast for an additional 45–60 minutes, depending on the size of the chicken, until:
    • The juices run clear when you prick the thickest part of the thigh, and
    • An instant-read thermometer inserted into the thigh (not touching bone) reads 165°F (74°C).

5. Rest the chicken

  1. Transfer the chicken to a warm platter and loosely tent with foil.
  2. Let rest 10–15 minutes before carving. This allows the juices to redistribute.

6. Make a simple pan sauce

  1. Pour off most of the fat from the roasting pan, leaving the browned bits (fond).
  2. Set the pan over medium heat on the stovetop.
  3. Add 1/2–1 cup chicken stock or water to deglaze, scraping up the browned bits with a wooden spoon.
  4. Simmer for a few minutes to slightly reduce and concentrate the flavor.
  5. Taste and adjust seasoning with salt and pepper. If desired, whisk in a small knob of butter at the end for a richer sauce.

7. Carve and serve

  1. Carve the chicken into legs, thighs, wings, and breast slices.
  2. Arrange on a warm platter.
  3. Spoon some of the pan juices/sauce over the chicken and serve the rest in a small sauce boat.
  4. Serve with roast potatoes, vegetables, or a simple green salad.
Tip: For extra crisp skin, leave the chicken uncovered in the refrigerator for an hour or two after salting the skin, then butter and roast as directed.
