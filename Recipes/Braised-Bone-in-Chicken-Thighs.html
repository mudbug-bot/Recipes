Braised Bone-In Chicken with Umami Pan Sauce

A favorite from Rob's recipe collection.

Ingredients

  • 4–6 bone-in chicken thighs and/or drumsticks
  • Salt (for seasoning; no pepper yet)
  • 1 Tbsp olive oil
  • 1 Tbsp butter
  • 1 small shallot or onion, finely sliced or diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, smashed
  • 1 Tbsp tomato paste
  • 1 tsp soy sauce
  • 1 tsp Worcestershire sauce
  • 1/2 cup chicken stock
  • 2 Tbsp cold butter, cubed (to finish the sauce)
  • 1 tsp fresh oregano, finely chopped
  • 2 Tbsp fresh parsley, finely chopped
  • Freshly cracked black pepper, to finish

Method

1. Season and dry

  1. Pat chicken completely dry.
  2. Season generously with salt only (no pepper yet, it will burn during searing).

2. Render and brown

  1. Heat olive oil in a skillet over medium heat.
  2. Place chicken skin-side down (or fattier side down for drumsticks) and do not move it.
  3. Brown deeply for 8–10 minutes.
  4. Flip and cook another 3–4 minutes, then remove chicken to a plate.

3. Build the umami base

  1. Lower heat.
  2. Add 1 Tbsp butter, shallot, and garlic; cook until softened.
  3. Stir in tomato paste and cook 1–2 minutes until it darkens slightly.
  4. Add soy sauce and Worcestershire.

4. Deglaze and braise

  1. Add chicken stock and scrape up all the browned bits (fond) from the pan.
  2. Return chicken to the pan, nestling pieces into the liquid.
  3. Cover loosely and simmer gently 20–30 minutes, until tender but still intact.
  4. Do not boil: maintain a lazy bubble to avoid dry, stringy meat.
  5. Alternatively, cover and transfer to a 300°F oven to braise gently.

5. Mount the sauce

  1. Remove chicken to a warm plate.
  2. Turn off the heat.
  3. Whisk in the cold butter cubes one at a time until the sauce is glossy and slightly thickened.

6. Finish and serve

  1. Spoon the sauce over the chicken.
  2. Finish with fresh oregano, parsley, and freshly cracked black pepper.
Pro Tip – What “mounting” a sauce means:
Whisking cold butter into a warm sauce off the heat creates a natural emulsion, softens sharp flavors, and gives the sauce restaurant-style body and gloss without heaviness.
