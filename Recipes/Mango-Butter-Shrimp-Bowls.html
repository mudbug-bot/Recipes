Creamy Black Beans with Smoky Bacon and Red Pepper

Ingredients

  • 1 lb bacon, sliced
  • ½ onion, small dice
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 red bell pepper, small dice
  • 2 (12-oz) cans black beans, drained and rinsed
  • Splash of beef stock (or water)
  • Butter, to finish
  • Ancho chili powder
  • Kosher salt
  • Fresh cracked black pepper

Method

  1. Render the sliced bacon in a wide pan until crispy.
  2. Pour off half the bacon fat (leave the rest — that's flavor).
  3. Add the onion and sauté until soft.
  4. Add garlic and cook just until fragrant.
  5. Add diced red pepper and sauté until tender.
  6. Stir in the black beans and a splash of stock.
  7. Simmer 5–8 minutes.
  8. Mash about half the beans against the side of the pan with a ladle to make them creamy.
  9. Finish with a knob of butter, ancho chili, salt, and fresh cracked pepper.

Charred Cherry Tomatoes

Ingredients

  • Long cherry tomatoes (or basic cherry tomatoes)
  • Olive oil
  • Sherry vinegar or balsamic
  • Kosher salt
  • Fresh cracked black pepper

Method

  1. Heat a carbon steel or cast iron pan until very hot.
  2. Add a touch of oil and toss in the tomatoes.
  3. Let them blister and lightly char — don't move them too much.
  4. Pull off heat and hit with a dash of vinegar, salt, and pepper.

Mango Butter Shrimp

Ingredients

  • 2 lbs shrimp (Argentinian red shrimp work beautifully)
  • Neutral oil
  • 4–5 cloves garlic, sliced or minced
  • 1 ripe mango, small dice
  • Butter
  • Lemon juice
  • Kosher salt

Method

  1. Heat a cast iron or carbon steel pan until blasting hot.
  2. Add oil, then the shrimp in a single layer.
  3. Sear hard on both sides until just cooked.
  4. Add the garlic and toast lightly in the shrimp fat.
  5. Splash in lemon juice.
  6. Add the diced mango and toss to coat.
  7. Finish with butter, mounting the sauce until glossy and rich.
  8. Season lightly with salt.

Assembly

  • Steamed rice on the bottom
  • Creamy black beans on one side
  • Mango butter shrimp over the top
  • Charred tomatoes scattered throughout
  • Fresh avocado pieces
  • Lime wedges
  • Cotija cheese
  • Fresh cracked pepper and a pinch of ancho or Aleppo chili to finish

Serve everything family-style and let people go to town. Bowls, tacos, second helpings — all encouraged.
