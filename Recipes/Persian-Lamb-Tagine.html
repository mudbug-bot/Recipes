Lamb

Persian-Lamb-Tagine

A favorite from Rob's recipe collection.

Ingredients

  • 2kg lamb neck fillets
  • 5 tbsp mild olive oil or sunflower oil
  • 3 medium onions, cut into thin wedges
  • 4 garlic cloves, finely chopped
  • 4 tsp ground cumin
  • 4 tsp ground coriander
  • 1 tsp hot chilli powder
  • 1 tsp ground turmeric
  • Large pinch of saffron
  • 2 cinnamon sticks
  • 300g ready-to-eat dried apricots
  • 250g ready-to-eat dried pitted dates
  • 100g shelled pistachios
  • 2 tsp rosewater
  • 25g cornflour
  • Small bunch coriander leaves, roughly chopped
  • Cooked couscous or basmati rice, to serve

Method

1. Brown the lamb

  1. Heat the oven to 180C/160C fan/gas 4.
  2. Trim any hard fat from the lamb and cut into chunks. Season all over.
  3. Heat 1 tbsp of the oil in a large flameproof casserole.
  4. Brown the lamb in 3–4 batches for 1–2 minutes until lightly coloured, adding 1 tbsp oil between batches.
  5. Transfer browned lamb to a bowl as you go.

2. Build the base

  1. Heat the remaining oil in the same dish over medium heat.
  2. Fry the onions for about 5 minutes until softened and lightly coloured.
  3. Stir in the garlic, cumin, coriander, chilli powder, turmeric, 1 tsp ground black pepper and 1 tsp flaked sea salt.
  4. Cook for 1 minute, stirring.
  5. Return the lamb to the dish and add 1.5 litres of water, the saffron, cinnamon sticks and lemons.
  6. Bring to a simmer, stirring a few times.
  7. Cover with a lid and transfer to the oven. Cook for 1 hour.

3. Add fruit and finish in the oven

  1. Remove the dish from the oven and stir in the apricots, dates and half the pistachios.
  2. Cover again and return to the oven for a further 30 minutes, or until the lamb is very tender.

4. Thicken and serve

  1. Move the dish to the hob and adjust seasoning to taste.
  2. Mix the cornflour with the rosewater and 3 tbsp cold water.
  3. Stir this into the tagine and cook over medium heat for 1–2 minutes until the sauce thickens.
  4. Roughly chop the remaining pistachios.
  5. Garnish the tagine with the nuts and coriander.
  6. Serve with couscous or basmati rice.
Freezer tip: Discard the cinnamon sticks before freezing. Thickened with cornflour, this tagine freezes well for up to 3 months and reheats nicely in the oven.
