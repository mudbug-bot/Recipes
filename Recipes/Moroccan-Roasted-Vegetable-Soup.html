Moroccan Roasted Vegetable Soup

Roasted root vegetables seasoned with ras el hanout get blended into a smooth, warming soup, finished with yogurt and mint if you like. It's a good way to turn a tray of caramelized veg into something you can ladle into a bowl.

Ingredients

  • 1 red onion, cut into 8 wedges
  • 300g carrot, cut into 2cm chunks
  • 300g parsnip, cut into 2cm chunks
  • 300g peeled butternut squash, cut into 2cm chunks
  • 1 small potato, cut into 2cm chunks
  • 2 garlic cloves
  • 1 tbsp ras el hanout
  • 1½ tbsp olive oil
  • 1.3l hot vegetable stock
  • 6 tbsp Greek-style yogurt and 1 tbsp finely chopped mint, to serve (optional)

Method

1. Roast the vegetables

  1. Heat the oven to 200C/180C fan/gas 6.
  2. Tip all the vegetables and the garlic into a roasting tin.
  3. Sprinkle over the ras el hanout and some seasoning.
  4. Drizzle over the olive oil and toss everything together.
  5. Roast for 30–35 minutes, turning halfway, until tender and lightly caramelised.

2. Make the soup

  1. Transfer the roasted vegetables to a large saucepan.
  2. Pour over the hot vegetable stock and simmer for 5 minutes.
  3. Purée the soup in a blender or with a hand blender until smooth.
  4. Adjust seasoning to taste.

3. Serve

  1. Ladle into bowls.
  2. Top with a dollop of Greek-style yogurt, a scattering of mint and a grind of black pepper, if you like.
Make-ahead: This soup holds up well in the fridge and reheats gently; just add a splash of stock or water if it thickens too much.
