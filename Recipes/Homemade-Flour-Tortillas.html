Homemade Flour Tortillas

Homemade-Flour-Tortillas • Makes 8 • Bread

Simple flour tortillas made with pantry ingredients and olive oil. They cook in minutes, puff on a hot pan, and make store-bought tortillas feel like a compromise.

Ingredients

  • 1½ cups all-purpose flour
  • ½ tsp baking powder
  • ½ tsp salt
  • ½ cup warm water
  • 3 tbsp + 1 tsp olive oil

Method

1. Make the dough

  1. In a bowl, mix the flour, baking powder, and salt.
  2. Add the warm water and olive oil.
  3. Knead for a few minutes until the dough is smooth and elastic.

2. Rest

  1. Divide the dough into 8 equal pieces and roll into balls.
  2. Let rest for 15 minutes to relax the gluten (this makes rolling easier).

3. Roll and cook

  1. On a lightly floured surface, roll each ball out thin.
  2. Heat a dry pan (cast iron works well) over high heat.
  3. Cook each tortilla about 45 seconds per side, until it puffs slightly and develops light brown spots.
  4. Stack cooked tortillas in a towel to keep warm and flexible.
Notes: If the dough springs back as you roll, let it rest another 5 minutes. Use these for tacos, quesadillas, wraps, or just tear and eat straight off the pan.
