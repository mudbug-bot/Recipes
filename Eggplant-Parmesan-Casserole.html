Eggplant Parmesan Casserole

Eggplant-Parmesan-Casserole • Serves 4–6 • Casserole

This eggplant parmesan casserole layers pan-seared eggplant slices with a garlicky marinara, a rich ricotta-Parmesan-pepperjack filling, and a crisp breadcrumb topping. It bakes into a bubbling, browned dish that eats like a cross between classic eggplant parm and a comforting baked casserole.

Ingredients

  • 2 large eggplants
  • 3 tablespoons olive oil, divided
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 pinch red pepper flakes
  • 2 cups marinara sauce
  • 2 tablespoons water
  • 1 cup ricotta cheese
  • 1 cup grated Parmesan cheese, divided
  • 1/2 cup pepperjack cheese, diced
  • 1 pinch salt
  • 1 pinch black pepper
  • 1/2 cup bread crumbs

Method

1. Prep and slice the eggplant

  1. Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Cut 5 crosswise slices from the center of each eggplant, about 1/2-inch thick, to total 10 slices.
  3. Peel and dice the remaining eggplant pieces and set aside.

2. Sear the eggplant slices

  1. Heat 2 tablespoons olive oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat until very hot.
  2. Lightly sprinkle the eggplant slices with a pinch of salt.
  3. Cook the slices in batches until slightly softened and lightly browned, about 3 minutes per side.
  4. Transfer the seared slices to paper towels to drain.

3. Make the sauce

  1. In the same skillet, heat the remaining 1 tablespoon olive oil.
  2. Add the minced garlic and cook for about 10 seconds, just until fragrant.
  3. Add the diced eggplant, a pinch of salt, and the red pepper flakes.
  4. Sauté until the diced eggplant is softened, about 5 minutes.
  5. Pour in the marinara sauce and water.
  6. Turn heat to medium-low and cook until the eggplant is tender, about 15 minutes, stirring occasionally. If the sauce gets too thick, add a little more water.

4. Make the cheese filling

  1. In a bowl, combine the ricotta cheese, 1/2 cup of the Parmesan, the diced pepperjack, a pinch of salt, and a pinch of black pepper.
  2. Stir until the mixture is thoroughly combined.
  3. Spread about 2 tablespoons of the cheese mixture over each seared eggplant slice.
  4. Fold each slice in half to enclose the cheese filling.

5. Assemble the casserole

  1. Spread about half of the sauce into the bottom of an 8x8-inch baking dish.
  2. Arrange the folded eggplant slices in a single layer over the sauce.
  3. Spread the remaining sauce evenly over the eggplant slices.

6. Make the topping

  1. In a small bowl, combine the bread crumbs, the remaining 1/2 cup Parmesan cheese, and 2 tablespoons olive oil.
  2. Mix until the crumbs are evenly coated and clump slightly.
  3. Sprinkle the crumb mixture evenly over the top of the casserole.

7. Bake

  1. Bake in the preheated oven until the casserole is bubbling and the topping is browned, 35 to 40 minutes.
Editor's note: If you are using a magazine version of this recipe that omits the breaded topping, be sure to include the crumb mixture here for the full casserole effect.
