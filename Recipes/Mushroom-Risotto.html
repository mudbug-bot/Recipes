Mushroom Risotto

A rich, creamy mushroom risotto with Arborio rice, wild mushrooms, cream, Parmesan, and fresh herbs. Also the base for Skewered Scallops over Mushroom Risotto.

Yield: 6–8 servings Category: Side Method: Stovetop

Description

This is a mushroom-forward risotto finished with cream, Parmesan, parsley, thyme, and black pepper. It stands on its own as a side or vegetarian main, and it also serves as the base for the scallop recipe that follows in the source book.

Ingredients

  • 5 Tbl unsalted butter
  • 3 Tbl minced shallots
  • 2 cups Arborio rice
  • 4–6 cups chicken stock, heated
  • 1 Tbl kosher salt, added to the chicken stock
  • 3/4 lb wild mushrooms (shiitakes, porcinis, morels, chanterelles, creminis, portobellos, oysters), cleaned and sliced
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 1/2 cup freshly grated Parmesan cheese
  • 2 Tbl chopped fresh parsley
  • 1 tsp chopped fresh thyme
  • 1 tsp freshly ground black pepper

Instructions

  1. In a very large skillet, heat 3 tablespoons of butter over medium heat and add the shallots. Cook until the shallots become soft.
  2. Add the rice and stir constantly so it does not brown. The grains of rice need to get hot.
  3. Add 1 1/2 cups of hot stock and lower the heat until the stock is barely simmering. Continue stirring constantly.
  4. As the stock is absorbed, add more stock in small amounts. Continue until the rice has become slightly tender.
  5. In a separate skillet, melt the remaining 2 tablespoons of butter over medium heat. Add the mushrooms and sauté until soft.
  6. Add the mushrooms to the risotto.
  7. When the rice is almost completely cooked, add the cream and stir slowly until absorbed.
  8. Remove from heat and stir in the cheese, fresh herbs, and pepper.
  9. Serve immediately.

Tips

  • Keep the stock hot so the risotto keeps cooking smoothly.
  • Add the stock gradually and let each addition absorb before adding more.
  • Serve as soon as it is finished for the best texture.
Note: Risotto is easy. Just take your time.
