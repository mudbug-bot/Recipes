Lamb and Apricot Stew

Lamb-and-Apricot-Stew • Serves 2 • Lamb

A quick, fruity Middle Eastern-style lamb stew built with ras el hanout, ginger, and soft dried apricots. It’s meant to be served over couscous with herbs and lemon for brightness.

Ingredients

  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 250g stewing lamb pieces
  • 1 onion, thinly sliced
  • 1 garlic clove, chopped
  • 1 tbsp chopped ginger
  • 2 tsp ras-el-hanout or other Middle Eastern spice mix
  • 1 tbsp tomato purée
  • 5 soft dried apricots, halved
  • 300ml vegetable or chicken stock
  • Cooked couscous, mint or coriander leaves, and lemon wedges, to serve

Method

1. Brown the lamb

  1. In a medium pan, heat 1 tbsp of the olive oil.
  2. Season the lamb pieces and fry briefly until browned.
  3. Remove the lamb from the pan and set aside.

2. Build the stew base

  1. Add the remaining 1 tbsp olive oil to the pan.
  2. Add the onion, garlic, and ginger with a little seasoning.
  3. Cook for about 5 minutes until softened.
  4. Stir in the ras el hanout, tomato purée, apricots, and stock.
  5. Return the lamb to the pan.

3. Simmer

  1. Bring to a gentle simmer and cook for about 25 minutes, or until the lamb is tender and the sauce slightly thickened.

4. Serve

  1. Serve the stew over warm couscous.
  2. Finish with mint or coriander leaves and lemon wedges on the side.
Serving idea: This makes a nice base for a small spread—add a simple salad and some yogurt on the side to round it out.
