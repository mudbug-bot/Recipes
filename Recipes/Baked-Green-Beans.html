Side Dish

Baked Green Beans

Crispy, tender oven-roasted green beans with garlic and parmesan — simple enough for a weeknight, good enough for company.

425°F Oven 18–22 Minutes Easy Side

Ingredients

  • 1 lb fresh green beans, trimmed
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1/4 tsp black pepper
  • 1/4 tsp red pepper flakes (optional)
  • 1/4 cup grated parmesan

Steps

  1. Preheat oven to 425°F. Line a large rimmed baking sheet with foil or parchment.
  2. Toss the green beans with olive oil, garlic, salt, black pepper, and red pepper flakes if using. Spread into a single layer so the beans roast instead of steam.
  3. Roast for 18–22 minutes, tossing once halfway through, until tender with lightly blistered, slightly crispy edges.
  4. Pull from the oven and immediately toss with grated parmesan. Serve hot.

Notes

Crispy Tips

  • Dry the beans thoroughly after washing — moisture is the enemy of browning.
  • Do not overlap them on the pan.
  • Use two sheet pans if needed.

Variations

  • Add slivered almonds in the last 5 minutes for crunch.
  • Finish with a drizzle of balsamic glaze.
If the pan looks crowded, split the beans between two sheet pans. Overcrowding is how crisp green beans turn into sad steamed beans.
Summary: Fast, garlicky, and finished with parmesan — this is the kind of side dish that quietly shows up and steals attention from the main course.
