Leek and Potato Soup, Two Ways

Julia Child • Classic French leek and potato soup, rustic or puréed

A simple, comforting soup of leeks, potatoes, and water or stock. Julia serves it two ways: rustic and chunky, or puréed and enriched with cream. Both versions start from the same base and are finished differently.

Ingredients

  • 3 cups (about 3 large) leeks, white and light green parts only, thoroughly cleaned and sliced
  • 3 cups (about 3 medium) potatoes, peeled and diced
  • 6–8 cups water or light chicken stock
  • 2–3 tsp salt (to taste)
  • Freshly ground black pepper
  • 2–4 Tbsp cream or butter (for finishing, optional)
  • 2–3 Tbsp chopped fresh chives or parsley (for garnish)

Instructions

1. Prepare the vegetables

  1. Trim the leeks, keeping only the white and light green parts. Split lengthwise and rinse thoroughly under running water to remove grit. Slice thinly.
  2. Peel the potatoes and cut into small dice or thin slices.

2. Simmer the soup base

  1. In a large soup pot, combine the sliced leeks, diced potatoes, and enough water or stock to cover them by about an inch (6–8 cups total liquid).
  2. Add 2 tsp salt to start.
  3. Bring to a boil, then reduce to a gentle simmer.
  4. Simmer uncovered for 30–40 minutes, until the vegetables are very tender and beginning to break down.
  5. Taste and adjust seasoning with more salt if needed.

3. Version 1: Rustic Leek and Potato Soup

  1. For a rustic, country-style soup, lightly mash some of the potatoes against the side of the pot with a spoon or potato masher, leaving much of the texture intact.
  2. Stir in 2–3 Tbsp of cream or a knob of butter if you like a richer soup.
  3. Add freshly ground black pepper to taste.
  4. Ladle into warm bowls and garnish with chopped chives or parsley.

4. Version 2: Puréed Creamy Leek and Potato Soup

  1. Let the base soup cool slightly.
  2. Working in batches, purée the soup in a blender until smooth, or use an immersion blender directly in the pot.
  3. Return the puréed soup to the pot and rewarm gently over low heat.
  4. Stir in 1/2–1 cup cream, depending on how rich you’d like it.
  5. Do not let the soup boil once the cream is added to avoid curdling; keep it at a gentle simmer.
  6. Season with salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste.
  7. Serve hot, garnished with chives or parsley and an extra drizzle of cream if desired.
Make ahead: The base leek and potato soup keeps well in the refrigerator for several days and can be reheated and finished either rustic or puréed just before serving.
