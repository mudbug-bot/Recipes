# BBQ Ribs

South Mississippi, my part of the country, has many excellent rib joints. Unlike the vinegar-based North Carolina version, our BBQ is sweet—some say it's all the pine pollen floating around in the air that makes us crave sweet stuff. Serve the ribs dry with a side of sauce. The rub works well for beef ribs, too.

## Yield
6–8 servings

## Ingredients
- 3 full racks of pork spareribs, 3–4 lbs each (3-inch/down)
- 2 cups white vinegar
- 1/2 cup paprika
- 1/4 cup garlic powder
- 2 Tbl onion powder
- 1 Tbl freshly ground black pepper
- 2 Tbl kosher salt
- 1/4 cup brown sugar
- 1/4 cup sugar
- 1 Tbl Creole Seasoning
- 1 recipe BBQ Sauce

## Instructions
1. Place the ribs in a large roasting pan or baking dish and pour the vinegar over the ribs.
2. Using your hand, rub all of the ribs with the vinegar and allow them to marinate for 1 hour.
3. Drain the vinegar and dry each rack completely with paper towels.
4. Combine the spices, sugars, and Creole Seasoning and coat the ribs completely with the mixture.
5. Cover and refrigerate overnight.
6. Prepare the grill.
7. Cook the ribs over indirect low heat for 2 1/2–3 hours or until they begin to pull away from the tips of the bones and the entire rack bends easily when held in the middle with a pair of tongs.
8. Serve ribs dry with BBQ Sauce on the side.

## Notes
- The dry rub also works well for beef ribs.
