Saffron Garlic Shrimp

Golden threads, bright garlic, and plump shrimp in a silky pan sauce
Serves
2–3
Cook Time
~15 min
Style
Seafood · French-Spanish
← Back to Recipe Index
📝 Shopping List
Ingredients
  • 12 jumbo shrimp, peeled & deveined
  • ½ tsp saffron threads
  • ¼ cup dry white wine (or water)
  • 4 garlic cloves, thinly sliced
  • 1 shallot, finely diced
  • 2 tbsp extra virgin olive oil
  • 1 lemon, cut into wedges for serving
  • 3 tbsp fresh parsley, chopped
  • Salt & black pepper
Instructions
  1. Heat the wine (or water) until just before boiling, transfer to a bowl, pinch in the saffron threads, and set aside to infuse.
  2. Pat the shrimp dry with paper towels, then season with salt and pepper.
  3. Heat a pan over medium heat and add the olive oil. After 2 minutes, add the sliced garlic and mix with the oil. 2 minutes later, add the diced shallot, season with salt and pepper, and stir.
  4. Add the shrimp seasoned side down. After 1 minute, flip them, then add the saffron-infused liquid and 2 tablespoons of parsley. Give the pan a quick shake.
  5. After the shrimp have cooked for a total of 2 minutes, remove them and transfer to a shallow bowl. Continue cooking the sauce for another 2 minutes to let it thicken up, then pour it over the shrimp and sprinkle with the remaining parsley.
  6. Serve with lemon wedges on the side, plus crusty bread, pasta, or rice.
Tips
  • Use high-quality saffron — it's the star of the dish and makes a huge difference
  • Wine adds depth and a subtle acidity; water keeps it lighter and lets the saffron shine through
  • Don't overcook the garlic or it will turn bitter; keep it at medium heat and watch carefully
