## Understanding Prime Rib

Prime rib is the dish, not the grade of beef.

USDA beef grades are based solely on marbling, the amount of intramuscular fat in the meat:
- Prime
- Choice
- Select

You do not need USDA Prime. A properly cooked choice-grade prime rib will be just as tender and flavorful.

## Portioning

- Boneless: 3/4 lb per person
- Bone in: 1 1/2 lbs per person

Always round up.

## Bone-in vs Boneless

### Bone-in
- The bone does not add flavor
- The bone insulates heat and helps even cooking

### Boneless
- Excellent option
- Must be tied for even thickness and doneness
- Use food safe butcher's twine.
- Tie every 1 1/2 to 2 inches
- Pull loose meat inward
- Create a uniform shape
- Even shape equals even cooking.

## The Salt Step (Most Important)

This is the most important step.

- Salt the roast generously on all sides 24 to 48 hours before cooking.
- Do not add garlic, butter, herbs, or spices yet.
- Salt is the only seasoning that penetrates meat.
- It denatures protein strands
- Allows proteins to retain more moisture
- Seasons the meat internally

This step makes prime rib juicier and more tender than any rub or marinade.

- Place the salted roast on a rack over a sheet pan and refrigerate uncovered.
- This dries the surface
- Forms a pellicle
- Improves browning during roasting

## Cooking Process

### First Stage
- Preheat oven to 225°F.
- Lightly coat the exterior with beef fat or neutral oil if desired.
- Roast until internal temperature reaches 115 to 120°F for rare to medium rare.
- Use a thermometer. Ignore the clock.
- Once the roast reaches temperature, it can rest up to 4 to 5 hours before finishing.
- This makes prime rib ideal for holidays and entertaining.

### Second Stage
- Increase oven temperature to 500°F.
- Apply a light compound butter:
  - Soft butter used as an adhesive
  - Thyme or chives
  - Mustard
  - Worcestershire
  - Optional miso

- Roast until browned and crusty.

## Final Temperature Guide
- 125°F medium rare ideal
- Adjust higher for more doneness
- Rest 20 minutes.
- Finish with black pepper and fresh herbs.
- Pepper belongs at the end.

## Making Gravy

Simmer beef scraps with onion, carrot, celery, garlic, and herbs.

Finish with a small cornstarch slurry for body.

Optional umami additions include Worcestershire, soy, or miso.

Aim for glossy, not thick.

## Final Thoughts

Prime rib is not difficult. It is misunderstood.

If you control salt, temperature, and timing, you will get restaurant-quality prime rib every time.

This method works for any large beef roast, not just holidays.
