Cacik

Turkish tzatziki-style yogurt and cucumber salad • Great with lamb

Pronounced "jajek" – a garlicky, minty yogurt and cucumber salad that’s especially good with lamb, but just as happy as a dip, sauce, or sandwich spread. Best eaten fresh so the garlic stays bright and the cucumbers don’t water it down too much.

Ingredients

  • 1 cucumber
  • 18 oz (about 2¼ cups) whole-milk yogurt
  • 1 tsp dried mint
  • 1–2 tsp salt
  • Leaves from 1 bunch fresh mint, chopped
  • 1–2 cloves garlic, depending on size and intensity
  • Extra-virgin olive oil, to drizzle

Method

  1. Peel and finely dice the cucumber.
  2. Add cucumber to a mixing bowl with the yogurt.
  3. Stir in dried mint, salt, and most of the fresh mint.
  4. Mince or microplane the garlic directly into the bowl and stir again.
  5. Transfer to a serving dish.
  6. Sprinkle the remaining chopped mint over the top and drizzle with olive oil.

Notes: Best served soon after making – over time the cucumber will release water and the raw garlic can turn harsh. Great as a side with lamb, as a dip, or as a mayonnaise substitute in sandwiches.
