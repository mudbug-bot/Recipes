Iraqi Lamb Kofta Kebabs

Iraqi-Lamb-Kofta-Kebabs • Serves ~6 • Lamb

These lamb kofta kebabs are heavily spiced and served over harissa-brushed aubergines, with hummus, salad, and flatbreads alongside. They’re built for grilling and serving family-style at the table.

Ingredients

For the kofta

  • 3 garlic cloves, crushed or grated
  • Thumb-sized piece of ginger, peeled and finely grated
  • 2 shallots, roughly chopped
  • Small handful of parsley, roughly chopped
  • 2 mint sprigs, leaves picked and roughly chopped
  • 2 tsp ground cumin
  • 2 tsp ground coriander
  • 1 tsp ground cinnamon
  • 1 tbsp tomato purée
  • 500g lamb mince
  • 1 tbsp chopped coriander
  • 2 tbsp pomegranate seeds
  • Hummus, fattoush salad, flatbreads and tzatziki, to serve (optional)

For the aubergines

  • 6 baby aubergines
  • 4 tbsp olive oil
  • 2 tsp ground cumin
  • 75g butter
  • 2 tbsp harissa

Method

1. Roast the aubergines

  1. Heat the oven to 180C/160C fan/gas 4.
  2. Slice the aubergines in half lengthways and score the flesh in a crosshatch, without cutting through the skin.
  3. Combine the olive oil and ground cumin, season well, and brush over the aubergines.
  4. Roast for about 45 minutes or until soft, then transfer to a platter.

2. Harissa butter

  1. Melt the butter in a small pan.
  2. Fry the harissa with a pinch of salt for a few minutes.
  3. Drizzle this over the cooked aubergines.

3. Make the kofta mixture

  1. Put the garlic, ginger, shallots, parsley, half the mint, cumin, coriander, cinnamon, tomato purée and 1 tsp each salt and freshly ground black pepper into a food processor.
  2. Blitz until everything is finely chopped, about 30 seconds.
  3. Tip the lamb mince into a large bowl and add the spice mixture.
  4. Stir until everything is well combined.
  5. If using bamboo skewers, soak them for 15 minutes.

4. Shape and grill

  1. Form rough handfuls of the lamb mixture into meatballs and thread onto skewers.
  2. Roll the meat in your hands to flatten into an egg-like shape along the skewer.
  3. Heat the grill to high and grill the kofta for about 6 minutes.
  4. Turn and cook for a further 6 minutes, or until cooked through.

5. Serve

  1. Arrange the kofta over the harissa aubergines.
  2. Scatter over the remaining mint, coriander and the pomegranate seeds.
  3. Serve with hummus, fattoush salad, flatbreads and tzatziki if you like.
Serving note: These kebabs are ideal for sharing platters—pile everything onto a big board and let people build their own plates with bread, salad, and dips.
